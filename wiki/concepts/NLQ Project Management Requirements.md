## Summary

This page captures the project management, organisation, workload, and experience requirements for the NLQ EPCIC 10 tender as specified in ITT §8.2.3.3 (Section 3: Management, Project Control and Administration), §8.2.3.2 (Project Execution Plan), §8.2.3.4 (Engineering), and Attachment 5 (Split of Responsibilities / Organisation). These requirements govern how TENDERER must document its planned project organisation, resource capacity, past experience, and management procedures.

## Key Points

### Split of Responsibilities — Attachment 5
- TENDERER must complete Attachment 5 specifying whether SERVICES will be performed with own resources only or with Consortium partners and subcontractors — ITT, §8.2.2(4), Att. 5
- For Consortium: all partners identified with name, office location, and scope responsibility; partnership agreement and sponsor declaration to be provided — ITT, Att. 5
- All Consortium partners jointly and severally bound to COMPANY; one partner nominated as sole agent (irrevocable for contract duration) — ITT, §8.2.2(4)
- Nomination of sole agent must include authority to: fully represent CONTRACTOR, act on behalf, receive payments, settle claims — ITT, §8.2.2(4)

### Organisation Chart Requirements
- TENDERER's proposed organisation chart(s) must identify management and all key personnel for each phase: engineering, procurement, construction, installation, offshore hook-up, and commissioning — ITT, §8.2.3.3(a)
- Chart shall identify key personnel by name; key personnel listed in Exhibit K — ITT, §8.2.3.3(a)
- Resumes of all identified key personnel in the contract organisation chart are mandatory — ITT, §8.2.3.3(b)
- For Engineering subcontract: Org chart shall detail positions of both Sub-CONTRACTOR and CONTRACTOR and be in line with the handover process — ITT, §8.2.3.4(f)
- General corporate/affiliate/project organisation chart required in PEP — ITT, §8.2.3.2(b)

### Mobilisation Schedule
- Mobilisation schedule(s) for the proposed contract organisation chart(s) required — ITT, §8.2.3.3(c)
- Manpower mobilisation histogram by phase, discipline, and location required — ITT, §8.2.3.3(c)

### Workload — Attachment 9 (Current and Potential Workload)
- TENDERER must complete Attachment 9 listing current workload: project name, client, progress %, contract value (MUSD), type of activity, peak manpower, role, award date, completion date — ITT, Att. 9
- TENDERER must also list potential workload: same fields plus planned award date — ITT, Att. 9
- TENDERER must identify roles dedicated to this project vs shared with other projects/departments — ITT, §8.2.3.3(d)
- TENDERER must provide current workload (man-hours) vs maximum workload capacity (man-hours) for each project phase, presented as histograms — ITT, §8.2.3.3(e)

### Past Experience — Attachment 10
- TENDERER must complete Attachment 10 providing past project experience in four categories: (1) contracts with TOTAL group, (2) contracts with QatarEnergy group, (3) contracts with other O&G companies, (4) contracts outside O&G — ITT, Att. 10
- For each entry: customer, designation of work/service, location, award date, completion date, contract value (MUSD), peak manpower, role (main contractor/JV partner/subcontractor), JV partners, major subcontractors — ITT, Att. 10
- Summary of work/service performed required for each entry — ITT, Att. 10

### Project Management Procedures
TENDERER must detail specific procedures to be used for management and control of the contract:
- Co-ordination procedure — ITT, §8.2.3.3(f)(xii)
- Risk Management Plan with procedure (including software to be used); initial identification of significant risks, severity estimation, and mitigation proposals — ITT, §8.2.3.3(f)(xiii)
- Reporting procedure with previous report examples — ITT, §8.2.3.3(f)(xiv)
- Cost Control procedure with software details — ITT, §8.2.3.3(f)(xv)
- Planning and Progress Measurement/Control procedure with software details — ITT, §8.2.3.3(f)(xvi)
- Documentation Management procedure with software details (final deliverables must be in electronic format compatible with COMPANY computer systems) — ITT, §8.2.3.3(f)(xvii)
- Communications Networks procedure with hardware/software details — ITT, §8.2.3.3(f)(xviii)

### Project Execution Plan (PEP)
- PEP is an executive summary document covering all project phases per Exhibit A Work Package structure (WP1–WP12, excluding WP2 HSE which is in Package C) — ITT, §8.2.3.2
- PEP must address at minimum: corporate/affiliate/project org chart; description of how TENDERER manages the WORK and interfaces with COMPANY; compliance statement on human dignity/fundamental rights per UN Declaration of Human Rights and ILO core principles; overall narrative for all phases — ITT, §8.2.3.2(b)
- PEP phases to cover: planning, coordination of disciplines, engineering management, procurement management, construction management, offshore hook-up management, T&I management, logistics management, onshore and offshore pre-commissioning/commissioning, interface management (external and internal) — ITT, §8.2.3.2(b)
- All activities on critical path must be clearly identified in PEP — ITT, §8.2.3.2(b)
- High-level project risks and mitigation strategies required — ITT, §8.2.3.2(d)
- Marine Warranty Surveyor acknowledgement statement required — ITT, §8.2.3.2(e)
- Digital approach plan required: preparation, implementation for E/P/C phases, training plan, 1D databases/2D schematics/3D models, AVEVA databases setup — ITT, §8.2.3.2(f)

### Engineering Execution Plan
- Full and detailed statement on engineering sequencing and methodology — ITT, §8.2.3.4(a)
- For engineering subcontract: address split of responsibility, document review cycle with two layers (CTR + Sub-CTR), proposed milestone for end of detail engineering, and handover process — ITT, §8.2.3.4(a)
- Engineering subcontract plan: MOA or MOU between main TENDERER and engineering sub-contractor for applicable disciplines — ITT, §8.2.3.4(b)
- Engineering office must cover all detail engineering competencies including AVEVA platform — ITT, §8.2.3.4(c)
- Engineering personnel details: capabilities, experience, and plan for dedicated resources — ITT, §8.2.3.4(e)
- Engineering schedule including manhours (for sub-contractor and contractor) — ITT, §8.2.3.4(g)
- List of engineering deliverables including preliminary EPC Master Document Register — ITT, §8.2.3.4(j)
- List of dedicated software to be used, in compliance with Exhibit A2 — ITT, §8.2.3.4(k)
- Specific engineering procedures: drawing control, internal discipline checks/reviews, change control, approval process, document status and distribution, weight control, final documentation — ITT, §8.2.3.4(l)

### Commissioning Organisation
- Commissioning organisation structured per OPERCOM® methodology with segregation between Construction/Pre-commissioning teams and Commissioning teams — ITT, §8.2.3.10(j)
- Commissioning Manager and all Commissioning team members dedicated full-time from Commissioning Engineering phase through project completion — ITT, §8.2.3.10(j)
- CVs of Commissioning Manager and key commissioning positions required for COMPANY approval — ITT, §8.2.3.10(j)
- Strong experience with OPERCOM® methodology and ICAPS© software required; ICAPS is mandatory for progress measurement — ITT, §8.2.3.10(c, d)

### Work Time Schedule
- Level 3 schedule required, consistent with Exhibit C (WORK TIME SCHEDULE); schedule covers all Work Packages per Exhibit A — ITT, §8.2.3.11(a)
- All critical path activities must be clearly identified — ITT, §8.2.3.11(c)
- Engineering schedule must include workshops: HAZID, HAZOP, Model Reviews, Constructability Reviews — ITT, §8.2.3.11(d)
- Manpower histograms by phase, discipline, and location; direct working hours histograms; man-hours split per phase — ITT, §8.2.3.11(f)
- Optional A-Location LQ schedule included under Scenario 1 (most cost-effective execution strategy) — ITT, §8.2.3.11(g)
