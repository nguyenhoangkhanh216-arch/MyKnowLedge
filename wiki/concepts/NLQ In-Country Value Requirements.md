## Summary
NLQ EPCIC 10 imposes formal In-Country Value (ICV) obligations under QatarEnergy's TAWTEEN initiative. CONTRACTOR must submit an ICV Plan at award, track it throughout execution, and achieve at least 70% of the committed score to receive the ICV Provisional Sum withheld by COMPANY.

## Key Points
- ICV Plan is mandatory at contract award; must follow TAWTEEN "ICV Plan Template Guidelines" (www.tawteen.com.qa). Plan covers 4 components: goods/services, workforce training, supplier development, investment in fixed assets. — Exhibit N, §§3–4
- **ICV Provisional Sum** is withheld by COMPANY and released pro-rata if CONTRACTOR achieves 70%–100% of the ICV Plan. Below 70% → Provisional Sum is forfeited entirely (or ICV Score set to 0% for future tenders for 6+ months). — Exhibit N, §§8.1, 9.1
- Provisional Sum amount is fixed at contract award and does NOT increase with Change Orders or scope growth; it may decrease proportionally if ICV Lump Sum decreases. — Exhibit N, §5.3
- Annual ICV Plan Tracker required if CONTRACT term > 1 year. Used to monitor in-year spend vs committed ICV Score; compared against ICV Plan at ICV Evaluation Date (final completion/delivery date). — Exhibit N, §6
- CONTRACTOR must appoint an ICV Representative as single point of contact with COMPANY on ICV matters. — Exhibit N, §5.4
- CONTRACTOR hires a CERTIFIER (QatarEnergy-approved) at its own cost to certify the final ICV Plan at ICV Evaluation Date. — Exhibit N, §6.5
- If scope changes impact the ICV Plan, CONTRACTOR submits an ICV Impact Assessment Report within 1 month; COMPANY accepts/rejects at sole discretion; failure to submit is deemed 0% achievement. — Exhibit N, §§7, 8.2
