## Summary

This page captures all Structural Engineering requirements for the NLQ EPCIC 10 project as defined in Appendix A1 §2.7. CONTRACTOR must design substructure, superstructure (topside including helideck, crane pedestal, telecom mast), and bridge (BI-BJ); perform all in-service and pre-service analyses using approved software (SACS, USFOS, MOSES, GRLWEAP, FEA); and design all appurtenances, installation aids, and PFP requirements per fire analysis.

## Key Points

### Structural Design Scope
- CONTRACTOR shall prepare all design basis and briefs, structural analyses, design and calculations per international codes and COMPANY Standards/Specifications — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall design: Substructure (including foundation, caissons, boat fenders, surfer landings); Superstructure (including helideck, crane pedestal, Telecom mast/tower); Bridge structure (BI-BJ) and bridge landing on NLQ side — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall develop all structural drawings for pre-service and in-service conditions; prepare all MTOs, specifications, material requisitions, TBE — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7

### In-Service Analyses
- CONTRACTOR shall perform all in-service analyses using software per §3: in-place, seismic, spectral fatigue, boat impact and post-impact, pushover (USFOS or SACS Advanced), helideck in-place, dropped object protection, natural frequencies, foundation design, vibration response, blast and fire — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall perform fire structural response analysis and design/locate PFP requirements per fire analysis — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall perform Fracture Mechanics Analysis (ECA) — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall evaluate and propose stressed skin design option for LQ Structure with pros/cons and cost savings compared to normal structural design — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7

### Pre-Service Analyses
- CONTRACTOR shall perform all pre-service analyses: weighing, load-out, towing, transportation (including transportation fatigue), lifting/launching, floatation/upending, floatover, pre-loading, on-bottom stability and mudmat design — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall perform pile drivability study using GRLWEAP including pile driving fatigue calculations (if applicable) — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall perform wind spectral fatigue and VIV check for bridges — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall perform vibration analysis of rotating equipment and specific piping lines; design vibration isolation systems and deck stiffness requirements — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7

### Substructure Details
- CONTRACTOR shall review geotechnical survey results and perform any re-assessment required; develop all soil properties/parameters and pile capacity curves for in-service and installation conditions — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- Rigid steel caissons shall be pre-installed inside substructure legs or protected against vessel impact; corrosion allowance of 6 mm for full length; coated with IM4 coating system — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall perform caisson support design and develop general arrangement drawings for anodes (cathodic protection) — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall develop detailed pile installation design and sequence including pre-identification of pile monitoring/hammering/drilling/grouting equipment requirements — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7

### Appurtenances and Miscellaneous
- CONTRACTOR shall design appurtenance items: mechanical handling systems, pipe racks, access/operating platforms, dropped object protection frames, crane pedestals (with fatigue checks), monorails, stairs, ladders, handrails — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall design all equipment supports based on VENDOR data — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall design special supports for piping, E&I cable trays and HVAC ducts — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall design construction supports, skid beams, pancake lifts, roll-ups, erection lifts, load-out supports, spreader beams/frames — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall develop Ampelmann access platform design — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall design jacking points for bridge bearing replacement during service — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall provide access to SACS, MOSES, USFOS, GRLWEAP, FE software calculation models to COMPANY monthly and organise review sessions prior to submission of critical calculations — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
- CONTRACTOR shall minimise hook-up and installation work offshore even if onshore work is slightly increased — Exhibit A App A1, §2.7
