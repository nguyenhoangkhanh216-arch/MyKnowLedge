## Summary

Topside weight management is the process of controlling the total structural weight of an offshore platform's topsides (above-water module) so it can be lifted onto the jacket by the designated crane vessel. If the factored topside weight exceeds the crane's lift capacity, the platform cannot be installed as designed — requiring either a weight reduction program, a topside split into multiple lifts, or a different crane vessel. In KMDD, both WHPs exceeded the Hoàng Sa crane's 900 MT capacity per WCR Rev.D1, triggering a design change directive in Jun-2026.

## Key Points

### Weight Metrics

- **Unfactored weight:** actual calculated mass of structural steel, equipment, piping, and outfitting
- **Factored weight:** unfactored weight × contingency factor — the figure compared against crane lift capacity
- **COG (Centre of Gravity):** must align within crane lift geometry; poor COG balance can disqualify a lift even within weight limits
- **WCR (Weight Control Report):** periodic formal report tracking cumulative topside weight vs target/limit

### KMDD Weight Overrun (WCR Rev.D1, 20-May-2026)

| Platform | Factored Weight (MT) | Crane Limit (MT) | Overrun (MT) |
|----------|---------------------|-----------------|--------------|
| **DD-WHP** | 992.1 | 900 | +92.1 MT |
| **KM-WHP** | 930 | 900 | +30 MT |

- Crane vessel: **Hoàng Sa** (VSP) — maximum 900 MT total incl. rigging; max 600 MT per hook
- DD-WHP is the critical case; KM-WHP overrun is smaller and addressed later
- Study commissioned 08-Jun-2026; design change officially actioned 10-Jun-2026

### Weight Shedding Measures (KMDD DD-WHP — actioned 10-Jun-2026)

1. **Cantilever deck cut** — separate Platform West module (~31.8 MT) as a distinct lift piece; installed offshore as Part 2
2. **Vent boom** — installed as a separate item offshore (not part of main topside lift)
3. **Solar panels & support structure** — relocated to Platform West slab; installed offshore; equipment rearranged East to clear No-Go Zone and escape routes; Tote Tanks moved East
4. **MEC item relocation** — all Main Deck MEC items shifted to Platform East during Load-Out and T&I to shift COG toward lifting point midpoint
5. **Counterweight** — 30 MT counterweight added at Platform East for COG balance

- Chemical injection (MEC-006) and other MEC items on Main Deck are affected by relocation to Platform East — skid layout and mounting changes needed
- Change Order impact assessment for CI system due **20-Jun-2026** (per EMT action items)
- Weight shedding changes must be incorporated into procurement specifications before TBE approvals
