## Summary

In EPCI contracts, the contractor has a contractual obligation to verify all FEED technical information within a defined period after the Effective Date (typically 30 days for IFR, 60 days for final). Any FEED errors or gaps not raised formally within this window cannot later be used as the basis for Change Orders. This makes the FEED Verification Register a commercially critical document, not just a technical exercise.

## Key Points

- **Purpose:** Contractor identifies discrepancies, errors, or gaps in FEED documentation that affect cost, schedule, or scope
- **Typical timeline:**
  - 30 days after Effective Date: Submit IFR (Issued for Review) FEED Verification Report
  - 60 days after Effective Date: Submit final FEED Verification Report
- **Commercial consequence of delay:** If the contractor misses the 30-day window, they lose the contractual right to claim Change Orders for issues they failed to report
- **FEED Verification Register:** A structured register of all FEED items reviewed, their status (confirmed/revised/hold), and any findings with cost/schedule impact
- **FEED Punch List:** Items from the client's FEED close-out report that require follow-up action; incorporated into the verification report as a separate section
- **Critical Findings sheet:** Items classified as Major/Moderate with cost/schedule impact — highest commercial sensitivity, requires all parties to input their findings
- **Parties typically involved:** Contractor PMC (lead), FEED contractor (VSP/NIPI type), client (COMPANY) for comment
- **KMDD timeline:** 30-day deadline = 09-May-2026 (COD for all parties to input findings by 07-May-2026)
