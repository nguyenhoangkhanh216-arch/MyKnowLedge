## Summary

Offshore topsides piping layout must satisfy both process/engineering requirements and physical installation constraints simultaneously. Space is tight, structures are fixed, and equipment elevations are set early in design. Common layout conflicts arise at drain systems, gravity lines, and pigging equipment, where slope requirements, clearances, and elevation constraints create design tradeoffs that must be resolved between Process and Piping disciplines — often requiring client approval.

## Key Points

- **Gravity/drain line slopes:** Process design criteria typically require 1:100 minimum slope for open drain and vent lines. Actual achievable slope depends on available elevation change between tie-in nozzle and collection point — often constrained by deck levels, equipment skid heights, and structural grating.
- **Pig trap area constraints:** Pig launchers and receivers are tall (trap body ~1200 mm above top of grating). This limits available elevation difference for drain connections below the trap, making standard 1:100 slope difficult to achieve without raising connection points impractically high.
- **Typical resolution path:**
  1. Piping team identifies constraint with evidence (model snapshot, elevation calculation)
  2. Raises in multidiscipline engineering meeting with Process and client
  3. Process reviews whether slope relaxation (e.g. 1:200) is acceptable for the specific service
  4. Agreement documented in design basis revision or TQ
- **Other common layout constraints:** Minimum valve stem clearances (150 mm between bottom flange and grating), instrument access space, pipe rack loadings, thermal expansion loops, and blast wall proximity
