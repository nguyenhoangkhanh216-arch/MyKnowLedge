## Summary

This page captures the onshore construction, fabrication, load-out, transportation, and installation requirements for the NLQ EPCIC 10 contract, drawn from Work Package 5 (Onshore Construction), Work Package 6 (T&I at Site), and Work Package 7 (Site Construction Works) of Exhibit A. CONTRACTOR bears full responsibility for the entire construction-through-installation lifecycle, including marine spread management, SIMOPS, and POB control.

## Key Points

### Onshore Construction (WP5)
- CONTRACTOR shall build, load-out, transport, install, hook up, commission, and start up the BI facilities in APPROVED sequence — Exhibit A, §11.1
- CONTRACTOR shall prepare all shop drawings, fabrication procedures, welding programmes, NDT plans, blast cleaning and painting procedures, as-built surveys — Exhibit A, §9.6
- CONTRACTOR shall compile a fabrication manual containing all AFC drawings, as-built drawings, APPROVED procedures, material control, welding, NDT, painting and coating — Exhibit A, §9.6
- Constructability reviews shall be conducted at least 3 months prior to start of WORK at WORKSITE with COMPANY attendance — Exhibit A, §9.6
- CONTRACTOR shall carry out onshore pre-commissioning per OPERCOM methodology (SD-NOC-EC-600, 601, 602); ICAPS software mandatory — Exhibit A, §11.7
- Piping Pressure Test Packs (flange management, flushing, pressure test, drying, reinstatement, preservation) shall be submitted to COMPANY for review and approval — Exhibit A, §9.8
- CONTRACTOR shall complete piping system pre-commissioning to maximum extent possible prior to module lifting and integration — Exhibit A, §9.8

### Marine Spread and T&I (WP6)
- All MARINE SPREAD shall be ISM certified, IACS classed, OVID-registered, and submitted for COMPANY and MWS inspection at least 4 months before mobilisation — Exhibit A, §4.4
- MARINE SPREAD above 20 years old requires extensive evaluation before acceptance — Exhibit A, §4.4
- MARINE SPREAD shall have IACS class-approved planned maintenance system and stability software — Exhibit A, §4.4
- Barge Master shall hold class 1 unlimited Master certificate (STCW-A-II/2) — Exhibit A, §4.4
- Final T&I documentation shall be approved by COMPANY and MWS at least 4 weeks prior to planned installation date — Exhibit A, §9.10.2
- CONTRACTOR shall provide data buoy for live measuring wind and wave parameters during T&I — Exhibit A, §9.10.2
- CONTRACTOR shall develop installation schedule, contingency plans, and Emergency Response Plan for each MARINE SPREAD vessel — Exhibit A, §9.10.2
- CONTRACTOR shall carry out dedicated HAZID for load-out, transportation, and offshore installation after IFA revision and before AFC revision — Exhibit A, §9.10.1

### SIMOPS and Site Constraints
- SIMOPS dossier shall be completed and ready for COMPANY review at least 4 months before offshore campaign; APPROVED at least 2 months before — Exhibit A, §4.6.2
- Pile hammering not permitted June–September due to Whale Shark Peak Aggregation season — Exhibit A, §4.6.1
- CONTRACTOR shall perform an EERA to cover SIMOPS and SHUTDOWN activities; justify required POB — Exhibit A, §4.6.1
- CONTRACTOR shall comply with COMPANY Process CP-NOC-063 and provide additional mitigations (cascade breathing systems, life rafts) to reach required CONTRACTOR POB — Exhibit A, §4.6.3
- All SITE WORK to be performed without production downtime as default; shutdown only when technically unavoidable and fully documented — Exhibit A, §4.6.5
- All offshore construction documentation (SIMOPS dossier, procedures, job cards, task sheets) must be COMPANY-APPROVED before offshore campaign begins — Exhibit A, §4.6.4
- CONTRACTOR shall carry out a HAZID for hook-up construction activities per location during detailed engineering — Exhibit A, §9.7

### Marine Warranty Surveyor (MWS)
- CONTRACTOR shall submit all detailed design documentation for MWS review on ongoing basis; no MARINE SPREAD operation under CAR policy shall commence without prior MWS written approval — Exhibit A, §7.5
