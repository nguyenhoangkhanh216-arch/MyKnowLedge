## Summary

This page captures all Architectural Engineering requirements for the NLQ EPCIC 10 project as defined in Appendix A1 §2.11. CONTRACTOR must perform detailed architectural engineering design for the full NLQ Living Quarter including all cabins, technical rooms, recreational spaces, medical centre, catering, helideck areas, and plumbing. All LQ spaces must be modelled in 3D and the design must integrate with structural, HVAC, fire protection, and escape route requirements.

## Key Points

### Architectural Design Scope
- CONTRACTOR shall perform detailed engineering design for: General Arrangement, Cabins with prefabricated integrated bathrooms, Technical Rooms, Utility Decks and Service Shafts, Working Areas, Changing Rooms, Wet Rooms, Laundry, Galley, Provisions Rooms, Dining — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- CONTRACTOR shall design: Muster Area, Recreation Areas (Main Recreation Lounge, Coffee Bar, TV Room, Library, Music Space, Cinema/Conference Room, Gymnasium, etc.), Medical Center, Prayer Rooms and Ablution — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- CONTRACTOR shall design: Catering Area, Heli Lounge and Heli Traffic Control, Plumbing and sanitary fixtures including piping diagram for plumbing — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- CONTRACTOR shall develop furniture and fittings for the NLQ Platform and provide all fitting, small appliances, equipment and furniture for safe and reliable operation of the Living Quarters — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- CONTRACTOR shall perform required studies and provide justification/technical note for material selection — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11

### 3D Modelling and Design Quality
- CONTRACTOR shall model the living quarters and technical rooms in the 3D model to as-built status — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- Design and engineering deliverables shall be developed to the required level of quality and detail to properly define the LQ and technical rooms — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- CONTRACTOR shall provide a safe and cost-efficient architectural design to facilitate installation and operation of equipment inside LQ and technical rooms — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11

### Fire, Blast, and Safety Integration
- CONTRACTOR shall perform LQ and Technical Rooms heat loss calculation and insulation thickness sizing — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- CONTRACTOR shall develop the fire-rated external walls design, internal partition, liner, ceiling and wall panel, fire-rated external doors and windows, internal doors, floors, and fire and acoustic insulations — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- CONTRACTOR shall develop blast wall design details if integrated with the LQ and technical rooms — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11

### Infrastructure Details
- CONTRACTOR shall develop the LQ and Technical Room wall penetrations dossier, MCT, HVAC ducts, plumbing, and other services details — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- CONTRACTOR shall develop: LQ and Technical Room escape route and warning/information signage, water supply and drainage system details, stairs and monkey ladder details — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- CONTRACTOR shall develop the layout of raised access floor, other flooring systems, and ceilings where applicable — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- CONTRACTOR shall develop the LQ and Technical Room Operation Manual — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- CONTRACTOR shall develop access/escape to and around the technical LQ building including doors — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
- All equipment and services within living quarters and technical rooms shall be harmonised for safe and reliable operation; first aid kit, safety kit, safety charts, warning signage, and key box shall be located at operable heights — Exhibit A App A1, §2.11
