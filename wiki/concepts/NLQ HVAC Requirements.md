## Summary

This page captures all HVAC Engineering requirements for the NLQ EPCIC 10 project as defined in Appendix A1 §2.5. CONTRACTOR must validate, model, and modify the HVAC DESIGN DOSSIER deliverables; develop the complete HVAC detailed design for the NLQ room programme; and perform all HVAC engineering through an APPROVED third-party subcontractor per Exhibit J. Design shall comply with COMPANY Standard SD-NOC-HVA-802.

## Key Points

- CONTRACTOR shall validate, model, verify and modify the HVAC Engineering deliverables and calculations listed in Exhibit E as well as develop the detailed design of the entire HVAC system — Exhibit A App A1, §2.5
- HVAC detailed design must cover all NLQ rooms: E&I and Telecom Technical rooms, Battery room, HVAC Plant room, Air locks, NLQ Cabins/Rooms/recreation area — Exhibit A App A1, §2.5
- CONTRACTOR shall perform all HVAC Detailed Engineering and fabrication documents through an APPROVED 3rd party subcontractor as defined in Exhibit J — Exhibit A App A1, §2.5
- The HVAC system design documents shall be developed in-line with COMPANY Standard SD-NOC-HVA-802 Appendix A2 and DESIGN DOSSIER — Exhibit A App A1, §2.5
- CONTRACTOR shall develop HVAC equipment specifications based on DESIGN DOSSIER and International codes and standards; responsible for any new equipment specification not covered by COMPANY specifications — Exhibit A App A1, §2.5
- CONTRACTOR shall prepare datasheets for: AHUs, Chilled Water Units, FCUs, Chilled Water Pumps, Fan units, HVAC Control Panel, Expansion Tank, Buffer Tank, Dosing Pot, all duct-in-line equipment and associated instruments — Exhibit A App A1, §2.5
- CONTRACTOR shall prepare the HVAC equipment list to be included in the Master Equipment List per §2.4 — Exhibit A App A1, §2.5
- HVAC design calculations shall be performed using the approved software listed in §3 (HAP Carrier for heat load calculations; AHH for psychrometric calculations) — Exhibit A App A1, §2.5
- 3D model of HVAC system shall include all HVAC equipment and supports, ducting, duct supports, duct in-line equipment, chilled water and refrigerant piping and supports, all HVAC instruments, electrical and instrument cable trays and supports — Exhibit A App A1, §2.5
- HVAC design deliverable shall comprise all information suitable for operation, maintenance, and troubleshooting — Exhibit A App A1, §2.5

### Performance Guarantee (from Exhibit D)
- HVAC system must prove cooling capacity, humidity, airflow rate, room pressurisation, and temperature per CONTRACTOR DOCUMENTS — Exhibit D, §4.2.2
- HVAC performance test duration is 1 month total: 20 days in summer and 10 days in winter, in offshore conditions — Exhibit D, §4.2.2
