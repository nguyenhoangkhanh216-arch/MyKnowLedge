## Summary

Personal Knowledge Management (PKM) is the practice of deliberately collecting, organizing, and connecting information to support thinking, learning, and decision-making over time. The goal is to build a personal knowledge base that compounds — where each new piece of information connects to existing knowledge rather than sitting in isolation.

## Key Points

- **Accumulation over retrieval:** Effective PKM builds structure progressively; the value is in the connections, not just the documents.
- **Common failure mode:** Systems abandoned when maintenance burden (filing, cross-referencing, keeping things current) exceeds perceived value.
- **RAG approach:** Most AI-assisted PKM today (NotebookLM, ChatGPT file uploads) re-derives knowledge on every query — no accumulation.
- **LLM Wiki approach:** LLM maintains all cross-references and summaries persistently; human curates sources and asks questions.
- **Obsidian as PKM tool:** Markdown vault with graph view, bidirectional links, and Dataview queries — well-suited for LLM-maintained wikis.
