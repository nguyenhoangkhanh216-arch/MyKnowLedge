## Summary

This page captures the Quality Assurance and Quality Control (QA/QC) submission requirements for the NLQ EPCIC 10 tender, as specified in ITT §8.2.3.12 (Package B Section 12) and Attachment 13. TENDERER must submit a Quality Management System certified to ISO 9001, a preliminary Quality Plan aligned with COMPANY Standard SD-NOC-PJC-501, and evidence of construction quality capability. These requirements govern QA/QC across all project phases (engineering, procurement, construction, installation, commissioning) and extend to subcontractors and vendors.

## Key Points

### QA/QC Submission Location in Tender
- QA/QC requirements are submitted as Section 12 of Package B (Technical Tender) and through completing Attachment 13 — ITT, §8.2.3.12
- Governing COMPANY standard: SD-NOC-PJC-501 (Quality Management); referenced standards also include SD-NOC-PJC-502 (Risk Management approach) and SD-NOC-PJC-503 (Supply chain quality surveillance) — ITT, §8.2.3.12

### Quality Management System (QMS) — Mandatory Submissions
- TENDERER's perennial Quality Manual shall be submitted — ITT, §8.2.3.12(a)
- ISO 9001:2008/2015 certificate of compliance from an accredited independent Third Party body, specifying scope, validity dates, and activities covered — ITT, §8.2.3.12(b)
- List of general project quality management procedures applicable to the contract, with objectives and field application for each — ITT, §8.2.3.12(c)
- Three mandatory procedures: (i) Control of project documentation and records, (ii) Internal audit procedure with list of qualified auditors, (iii) Management of change procedure — ITT, §8.2.3.12(d)

### Quality Manual Evaluation Focus Points
COMPANY will evaluate the Quality Manual against the following criteria:
- Relevance of TENDERER's Quality Policy with COMPANY's own Quality Policy — ITT, §8.2.3.12(e)(i)
- Level of Top Management commitment to quality — ITT, §8.2.3.12(e)(ii)
- Practicality of the QMS for activities relevant to the WORK — ITT, §8.2.3.12(e)(iii)
- Relevance of supporting documents listed in Exhibit A Appendix A2 — ITT, §8.2.3.12(e)(iv)
- Effectiveness of continuous Quality Improvement process evidenced by quantified KPIs — ITT, §8.2.3.12(e)(v)

### Preliminary Quality Plan — Mandatory Submissions
- Preliminary Quality Plan outlining the QMS to be implemented for each phase of the WORK, in line with Exhibit H and SD-NOC-PJC-501 — ITT, §8.2.3.12(f)(i)
- Plan shall include data for all JV/Consortium partners and main subcontractors — ITT, §8.2.3.12(f)(ii)
- Quality organisation chart for each project phase with interfaces to TENDERER's corporate Quality organisation; duties and responsibilities of Quality and management key positions — ITT, §8.2.3.12(f)(iii)
- Plan must demonstrate full understanding of the particularities of this WORK; existing plan from a similar project is deemed inadequate if not customised — ITT, §8.2.3.12(f)(iv)
- Plan must cover subcontractor and vendor assessment procedure prior to subcontracting or ordering, plus subcontract and vendor quality surveillance — ITT, §8.2.3.12(f)(v)
- Submission of Preliminary Quality Plan does not relax obligation to issue further quality deliverables for COMPANY review at later stages — ITT, §8.2.3.12(f)(vi)

### Construction Quality Experience Evidence
- Typical Quality KPI data for last 3 years — ITT, §8.2.3.12(g)(i)
- Past experience on structural steel fabrication using EN10025 S420 or equivalent steel grade — ITT, §8.2.3.12(g)(ii)
- Number and capacities of CNC machines available in workshop; if subcontracted, provide subcontractor details — ITT, §8.2.3.12(g)(iii)
- Welding production capacity (number of welders and machines) and material range experience (qualified and produced) — ITT, §8.2.3.12(g)(iv)

### QC and Supply Chain Surveillance
- TENDERER shall acknowledge understanding of Exhibit H and SD-NOC-PJC-503 using a Risk Management approach per SD-NOC-PJC-502 — ITT, §8.2.3.12(h)
- If external Inspection Agencies are used, they must be identified in the statement — ITT, §8.2.3.12(h)

### Certification and Regulatory Compliance
- TENDERER shall acknowledge understanding of certification requirements in Exhibit H — ITT, §8.2.3.12(i)
- TENDERER shall list all permits, licenses, approvals, notifications, and associated laws and regulations relevant to the WORK — ITT, §8.2.3.12(j)

### Attachment 13 — QA/QC Questionnaire
TENDERER must complete the Attachment 13 questionnaire and provide supporting documents on:
- Company-wide quality policy addressing QA and QC — ITT, Att. 13
- Use of specialized consultants for QMS development — ITT, Att. 13
- Company Quality Manual and suite of QMS procedures — ITT, Att. 13
- Company Quality Plan maintenance — ITT, Att. 13
- ISO 9001 certification version, issuing authority, and scope (including uncovered business units) — ITT, Att. 13
- Most recent independent audit findings, anomalies, or non-conformances — ITT, Att. 13
- Senior QA/QC Manager position, reporting line, and authority — ITT, Att. 13
- Other quality improvement programmes and standards implemented — ITT, Att. 13

Required attachments to Attachment 13: policy statements, QA/QC organisation chart, QA Manual summary, Quality Plan summary, quality certificates (ISO, API, etc.) — ITT, Att. 13

## Exhibit H Full QA/QC Framework (Contract Phase)

### QMS Structure — Two-Component System
- CONTRACTOR must operate two QMS components: (1) perennial company QMS certified to ISO 9001:2015, and (2) contract-specific QMS detailed in a Project Quality Plan (PQP) — Exhibit H, §3
- ISO 29001:2020 accepted as oil and gas sector alternative to ISO 9001:2015 — Exhibit H, §3.1
- PQP supported by Quality Control Plan(s) (Inspection and Test Plans / ITPs) — Exhibit H, §3.2

### PQP Submission Timeline
- Offer stage: preliminary PQP + Quality Manual + ISO 9001 certificate + documented procedures (doc control, internal audit, MOC) — Exhibit H, §4.2.1
- Within 30 days of EFFECTIVE DATE: intermediate PQP + preliminary Quality Control Plan(s) for COMPANY review — Exhibit H, §4.2.2
- Within 60 days of EFFECTIVE DATE: final PQP + first Quality Control Plan(s) with COMPANY surveillance points for COMPANY APPROVAL — Exhibit H, §4.2.3
- Annual PQP revision minimum; all modifications require COMPANY APPROVAL prior implementation — Exhibit H, §4.2.4

### Quality KPIs — Mandatory Contractual Targets
- Corrective Action Requests from COMPANY audits: closed within 3 months — Exhibit H, §4.1
- NCR closure: 90% within 60 days — Exhibit H, §4.1
- Punch list closure rate: 95% at Sail Away — Exhibit H, §4.1
- Weekly rolling 4-week KPI reporting: welding repairs, welder rejection rates (structural/piping/pipeline), NDT backlogs — Exhibit H, §4.1
- KPIs reviewed at weekly QAQC Meetings, Monthly Report, and 6-monthly Project Management Review — Exhibit H, §4.1

### NCR, Deviation, and Concession Procedures
- NCR: CONTRACTOR must respond within 5 days stating prevention/remedial work; major non-conformities require COMPANY APPROVAL before incorporation — Exhibit H, §4.3.10
- Non-conforming items must be quarantined and tagged immediately; no release until NCR closed and APPROVED — Exhibit H, §4.3.10
- QER (Quality Event Report): simplified form for minor site defects; escalates to NCR if no improvement — Exhibit H, §4.3.10
- Deviation Request (DEV): must be issued PRIOR to deviating from COMPANY requirements; signed by Quality Representative — Exhibit H, §4.3.5
- Concession Request: for post-occurrence acceptance of non-conforming products; requires COMPANY written consent; no non-conforming product incorporated without consent — Exhibit H, §4.3.10
- NCRs, Concession Requests, Audit reports: submitted through EDMS - MANASA — Exhibit H, §4.3.10 and §4.3.14

### CRA Materials — Al Shaheen Specific Requirements
- Dedicated segregated workshop required for all CRA fabrication (storage, cutting, bending, welding, inspection); physically separated from any ferrite contamination — Exhibit H, §4.3.3 and §5.12.1
- 100% Positive Material Identification (PMI) on all CRA components and welds during prefabrication and as-installed pre-commissioning — Exhibit H, §5.12.1
- CRA material substitution via DEV process with CSCC (Chloride Stress Corrosion Cracking) technical justification — Exhibit H, §5.12.1
- CRA coating per SD-NOC-COR-350 — Exhibit H, §5.12.1

### Ex Equipment — TPI Audit Requirement
- CONTRACTOR must mobilise a CA-approved third party to audit Ex Equipment installation for IEC 60079-14 compliance during construction — Exhibit H, §5.12.2

### Quality Control — Surveillance Point Categories
- Four categories: Review (R) for document phases; Monitoring (M) for ongoing surveillance; Witness Point (W) for notified activities (absence does not suspend); Hold Point (H) for notified activities (execution suspended if COMPANY absent) — Exhibit H, §5.3
- Design review, FMA, HAZOP, HAZID: classified as Hold Points — Exhibit H, §7.2
- Inspection notification: 15 days (KOM/PIM, foreign country); 7 days (in-country); 2 working days (at WORKSITES) — Exhibit H, §5.8
- 6-week look-ahead inspection/surveillance programme required — Exhibit H, §5.8
- Inspection reports issued within 2 working days via NOC ShareFile platform — Exhibit H, §5.9

### Subcontractor/Vendor Quality Requirements
- Pre-qualification dossier required for any new vendor not on APPROVED list: includes ISO 9001 cert, QMS, HSE KPIs (3 years), financial data, anti-corruption policy — Exhibit H, §6.1
- Criticality Rating 1 and 2 items: pre-inspection meeting (PIM) mandatory; COMPANY entitled to attend — Exhibit H, §6.3
- New/recently approved vendors: treated at Criticality Rating 1 level — Exhibit H, §6.3
- Equipment without correct documentation and tagging on arrival at WORKSHOP: quarantined as non-conforming — Exhibit H, §6.5

### Quality Organisation — Personnel Requirements
- Project Quality Manager is key personnel per Exhibit K; requires COMPANY APPROVAL — Exhibit H, §9.1
- Audit technical experts: NDT Level III, IWE/EWE Welding Engineer, FROSIO/NACE Level III Coating, CompEx Electrical — minimum 10 years experience each — Exhibit H, §4.3.14
- Process audits required for: NDT, Welding, Coating, GRP/GRE, Electrical installation, Flange Management — minimum 1 auditor + 1 expert full-time at each site — Exhibit H, §4.3.14
- Minimum 1 QMS audit covering entire scope every 6 months — Exhibit H, §4.3.14
- Piping/Flange personnel: trained and recorded in dedicated register with unique ID + hard hat sticker — Exhibit H, §9.3.2
- Ex Equipment inspectors: IEC 60079-certified; recorded in register with unique ID — Exhibit H, §9.3.3

### Quality Dossier — Final Documentation
- Quality Dossier (Volume 2) maintained from ED to CONTRACT completion; 61 document categories per Table 10-1 — Exhibit H, §10
- Vendor Final Dossier due within 1 month after FAT — Exhibit H, §10.1
- Quality Record Book index submitted to COMPANY within 6 weeks after EFFECTIVE DATE — Exhibit H, §10.2
- Structure drawings must include weld identification and material certificate numbers for full material traceability — Exhibit H, §10.1

### Certification Framework (App H1)
- Safety/lifting equipment (HIPPS, SIL, FFE): Certifying Authority (CA) at all phases E/P/I/C — Exhibit H App H1, §6
- Main bearing structure (jacket, primary topside): CA for E/P/I; QC only for C — Exhibit H App H1, §6
- Critical production equipment (rotating machines, HP vessels): CA for E/P; QC for I/C — Exhibit H App H1, §6
- Qatar Decree Law No. 4 of 1977: CA must issue 4 conformity certificates (Design; Procurement/Fabrication/Construction; Installation; overall PLANT) from Qatar-based branch — Exhibit H App H1, §7

### Material Certification Types (App H2)
- Type A (EN 10204 3.2 CA-endorsed): CRA, sour/lethal service, First/Special Category steel, riser piping, pipeline components — Exhibit H App H2, §4 and Table 6-1
- Type B (EN 10204 3.1 vendor-issued with test results): Second Category steel, instruments, welding consumables — Exhibit H App H2, §4
- Passive fire protection: fire test certificate from nationally accredited lab or CA-witnessed test required — Exhibit H App H2, §5.2.4
- Cranes: critical weld re-NDT after Proof Load Test by TPI before Sail Away — Exhibit H App H2, §5.2.19
- Pressure vessels: ASME VIII code compliance certificate + CA suitability certificate — Exhibit H App H2, §5.2.15

### Criticality Ratings — Key Equipment Ratings (App H4)
- Criticality 1 (Highly Critical — Level I full inspection): ICSS system (all cabinets), Wellhead Control Panel, GTG, Emergency Diesel Generator, WAG Compressor, all permanently installed lifting ≥10MT — Exhibit H App H4, §4–5
- Criticality 2 (Critical — Level II occasional): HVAC system (all components), HV switchgear, UPS, choke valves, PSVs, control valves, MOVs, PA/GA system, LAN/Telecom, CCTV, firewater pump, deluge valves, crude export pumps — Exhibit H App H4, §5
- Criticality 3 (Moderately Critical — Level III final inspection): most motors, controllers, transmitters, gauges, HVAC fans — Exhibit H App H4, §5
- Criticality 4 (Not Critical — Level IV document only): personal safety equipment, most fire portables, lighting, socket outlets — Exhibit H App H4, §5

### Interface Items Handover (App H5)
- Preliminary Interface Items delivery list issued at ED+2 months; updated monthly — Exhibit H App H5, §3.1.1 and §5.1.1
- Category A items (tagged equipment): release inspection at CONTRACTOR's In-Country Warehouse; 15 days COMPANY notice — Exhibit H App H5, §5.1.5
- Category B items (untagged bulk: bolts, gaskets, pipes, cables): inspection before shipment from WORKSITE; 2 working days COMPANY notice — Exhibit H App H5, §5.1.5
- Items shipped directly from VENDOR to Qatar cannot be Category B — Exhibit H App H5, §5.1.2
- Dispatch Dossier per item using SAP QC Codes; COMPANY APPROVAL required before Release Inspection notification — Exhibit H App H5, §5.1.4
