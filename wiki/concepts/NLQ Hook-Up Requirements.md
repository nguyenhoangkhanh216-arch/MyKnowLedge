## Summary

This page captures all Hook-Up Engineering requirements for the NLQ EPCIC 10 project as defined in Appendix A1 §2.12, with supplementary requirements from Exhibit A Work Unit 3.6. CONTRACTOR must minimise offshore hot works and manhours; prepare a Hook-Up manual with all AFC drawings and procedures; define all work packs to job card level; and manage all work pack dossiers through an in-house database linked to the project schedule tool. Hook-up engineering is critical for SIMOPS risk management.

## Key Points

### Hook-Up Engineering Principles
- CONTRACTOR shall optimise design with the objective of minimising the amount of hot works and manhours spent offshore as well as reducing costs — Exhibit A App A1, §2.12
- CONTRACTOR shall prepare a Hook-Up manual containing all documents and pertinent information related to hook-up at SITE of BJ/BI — Exhibit A App A1, §2.12
- Hook-up manual shall include: as-built drawings, hook-up AFC drawings, survey reports, APPROVED procedures related to SITE and WORKSITE WORK including weather limitations — Exhibit A App A1, §2.12
- CONTRACTOR shall define the exhaustive list of all Work Packs to complete offshore hook-up works per the Offshore Hook-up and Construction Work Execution Plan, including assistance to commissioning — Exhibit A App A1, §2.12
- CONTRACTOR shall identify associated critical constraints, priorities and sequences — Exhibit A App A1, §2.12
- CONTRACTOR shall identify and study modifications necessary on existing facilities to accommodate CONTRACTOR Marine Spread — Exhibit A App A1, §2.12

### Work Pack Requirements
- CONTRACTOR shall prepare all Work Packs per location, working condition (normal or SIMOPS), offshore campaign phase, and discipline with minimum contents: Detailed Scope of Work, description of elementary tasks per discipline and CONSTRUCTION EQUIPMENT, consolidated workmanship resource histogram, Detailed Schedule, personnel list, material list, engineering documents (P&IDs, drawings, isometrics), and Job Cards — Exhibit A App A1, §2.12
- CONTRACTOR shall manage the Work Pack dossier through in-house database associated with PROJECT schedule tool and CONTRACTOR Management System — Exhibit A App A1, §2.12
- All documents shall be incorporated into the specific database used for reporting, identification of critical path schedule, and programme by task with follow-up — Exhibit A App A1, §2.12

### From Exhibit A WU 3.6
- CONTRACTOR shall perform full engineering studies (including stress analysis) to design all hook-up systems: existing firewater system, electrical, instrumentation (tubing, cables, supports), diesel transfer, F&G equipment from Junction Boxes, hydraulic/air distribution panel to valves — Exhibit A, §9.7
- CONTRACTOR shall perform procurement engineering for all identified hook-up material — Exhibit A, §9.7
- CONTRACTOR shall issue all associated Job Cards for performance of Hook-up and Integration works — Exhibit A, §9.7
- Each offshore activity shall be subject to COMPANY Permit to Work (PTW) system per CP-NOC-032; PTW shall be issued at least 72 hours prior to start of WORK — Exhibit A, §9.7
- During detailed engineering, CONTRACTOR shall carry out dedicated HAZID review for hook-up construction activities per location — Exhibit A, §9.7
- CONTRACTOR shall minimise the amount of offshore work by optimising onshore pre-fabrication work (WU 3.5) — Exhibit A, §9.7
