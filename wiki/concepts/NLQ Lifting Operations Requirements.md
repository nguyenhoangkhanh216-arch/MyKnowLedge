## Summary

This page consolidates NLQ EPCIC 10 lifting requirements from both Appendix A5 (Lifting Appliances Documentation) and Appendix A6 (Lifting Operations Management). App A5 covers the mandatory certification and documentation for cranes and winches. App A6 covers the management system, competence requirements, planning processes, criticality classification, and inspection intervals.

## Key Points

### Lifting Appliances Documentation — Cranes (A5)
- 18 mandatory crane documentation items including: Design calculations and structural drawings; Third party load test certificate; Load chart, radius derating chart; Wire rope test certificates; Slewing ring, hooks, sheave blocks certificates; NDT reports; Rope drums, brakes, controls certificates; Operating manual; Certificate of Compliance; Stability and grillage design — Exhibit A App A5, §2
- Reference standards for crane documentation: SD-NOC-STR-401 (Structural design of cranes and lifting equipment), SD-NOC-STR-411, SD-NOC-INP-IM-305, SD-NOC-LH-003 — Exhibit A App A5, §2

### Lifting Appliances Documentation — Winches (A5)
- 10 mandatory winch documentation items including: Design calculations; Load test report; Wire rope test certificate; Proof load certificate; Operating manual; Certificate of Compliance; Hook certificate — Exhibit A App A5, §3

### Lifting Management System (A6 §3)
- CONTRACTOR shall conduct a gap analysis of its Lifting Management System against COMPANY requirements and submit the analysis report — Exhibit A App A6, §3
- CONTRACTOR must comply with COMPANY's Lifting Management System and NOC Lifting Standard — Exhibit A App A6, §3
- CONTRACTOR shall designate a Competent Person in Lifting Operation (CPLO) at each worksite and offshore; CPLO is responsible for all aspects of lifting activities — Exhibit A App A6, §3.2

### Lifting Planning (A6 §4)
- Critical lift list must be submitted to COMPANY 60 days in advance for activities at Contractor WORKSITE; 90 days in advance for activities at COMPANY SITE — Exhibit A App A6, §4
- 12-week rolling lifting plan must be submitted to COMPANY weekly for review — Exhibit A App A6, §4
- All lifting operations must be pre-planned; improvised lifts are not permitted — Exhibit A App A6, §4

### Criticality Assessment (A6 §5)
- Criticality is determined by a 26-question assessment matrix; CONTRACTOR assesses each lift and classifies as critical or non-critical — Exhibit A App A6, §5
- Non-critical lift requires: Lifting Supervisor, Signaller, Certified Rigger(s), Competent Operator; Lifting Plan Routine (LPR) document — Exhibit A App A6, §5
- Critical lift requires all non-critical requirements PLUS: Written Critical Lift Plan; COMPANY Approval; Attendance of CPLO and COMPANY representative during execution — Exhibit A App A6, §5

### Inspection Intervals (A6 §6)
- All lifting equipment must be inspected by a Competent Person at maximum 6-month intervals — Exhibit A App A6, §6
- Thorough examination by a COMPANY-approved third-party inspector required at maximum 12-month intervals — Exhibit A App A6, §6
- All lifting equipment must have a current certificate of fitness before use — Exhibit A App A6, §6

### Personnel Baskets (A6 §7)
- Personnel transfer baskets must comply with: BS EN 14502-1; ASME B 30.23; API RP 2C; BS EN 13852 — Exhibit A App A6, §7
- Personnel basket lifts are always classified critical lifts — Exhibit A App A6, §7
