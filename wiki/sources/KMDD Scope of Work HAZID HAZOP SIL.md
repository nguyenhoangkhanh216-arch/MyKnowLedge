## Summary

Defines the methodology, scope, deliverables, and schedule requirements for HAZID, HAZOP, and SIL Classification studies during the KMDD Detailed Engineering phase. Covers both KM-WHP and DD-WHP platforms and associated pipelines. Document KDP-OOO-03-SW-X-0004-E0 issued for Approval (IFA) 01-Jun-2026.

## Key Points

### Project Context
- Two unmanned wellhead platforms (KM-WHP, DD-WHP), 4 conductor slots each
- SIPROD (Simultaneous Production and Drilling) capability required
- Platform design life: 10 years (topsides), 15 years (structure)
- Water depth: ~53 m; Location: 205 km SW of Cape Ca Mau, Block 46/13
- FWS pipeline: 20" × 6.7 km (KM-WHP to DD-WHP); 20" × 22.0 km (DD-WHP to BK-C)
- First Gas target: Q4 2027; EPCI LOI: 30-Mar-2026

### Study Methodology
- **HAZID:** Per ISO 17776 — covers normal operations, SIMOP, SIPROD; 2 working days
- **HAZOP:** Per IEC 61882 — all operating phases (start-up, shutdown, maintenance); 4 working days
- **HAZOP Vendor Packages** (WHCPU, HPU, Chemical Injection): 3 working days
- **SIL Classification:** LOPA method per IEC 61508/61511; 3 working days
- No risk ranking during HAZOP — assessment matrix for reference only
- Workshops at CONTRACTOR's office, Tam Thang ward, Ho Chi Minh City

### Deliverables & Schedule
- Terms of Reference (TOR) submitted 2 weeks before workshop
- All P&IDs, PFDs, cause-and-effect diagrams, and datasheets provided prior to workshop
- Reports in 4 stages: Draft → IFR → IFA → AFC
- Chairman CV approval required before workshops; min. 12–15 years experience; CFSE or Functional Safety Engineer certified
- Facilitated by TÜV Rheinland (per actual study reports)

### Scope of Equipment Covered
- All process systems (wellheads, manifolds, closed drain, vent, open drain)
- Chemical injection systems (corrosion inhibitor, PPD, scale, methanol)
- Utility systems (diesel, nitrogen, service water, firewater, generator)
- Safety shutdown systems and HIPPS
- Vendor packages: WHCPU (Wellhead Control Panel Unit), HPU, CI skids
