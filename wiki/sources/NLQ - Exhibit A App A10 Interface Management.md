## Summary

Appendix A10 (Rev A) defines CONTRACTOR's interface management obligations for the NLQ EPCIC 10. Interface management covers all CONTRACTOR activities from Effective Date to Completion Certificate issuance. COMPANY retains overall responsibility for interface management but CONTRACTOR must identify, track, and manage all project interfaces.

## Key Points

### Scope and Principles (§1–§2)
- Interface organisation and identification are within CONTRACTOR's scope; follow-up and performance of identified tasks related to interfaces are integral to CONTRACTOR's scope of WORK — Exhibit A App A10, §1
- Interface management covers all CONTRACTOR activities: engineering, procurement, fabrication, pre-commissioning, transportation, installation, hook-up, and commissioning — Exhibit A App A10, §1
- CONTRACTOR shall ensure early identification of interfaces in accordance with scope and battery limits — Exhibit A App A10, §2
- CONTRACTOR shall collaborate with all parties involved in interface activities, channel communications through COMPANY, and ensure prompt/reliable/traceable information exchange — Exhibit A App A10, §2
- COMPANY shall always remain responsible for overall management of interfaces — Exhibit A App A10, §2

### Interface Management Process (§3)
- CONTRACTOR shall implement appropriate procedures and submit to COMPANY for APPROVAL: Interface Plan, schedule, Interface Coordination Procedures, overall and detailed drawings/tables showing physical interfaces — Exhibit A App A10, §3
- CONTRACTOR shall organise Interface kick-off meeting within the first month after contract award and subsequent weekly Technical Interface Coordination meetings — Exhibit A App A10, §3
- CONTRACTOR shall designate a focal point (Interface Leader) for all interface matters with clearly defined roles and responsibilities — Exhibit A App A10, §3
- CONTRACTOR shall provide a follow-up and reporting tool on all interface-related matters; critical items reported in project monthly report — Exhibit A App A10, §3
- CONTRACTOR shall formally advise COMPANY of any interface problems that arise — Exhibit A App A10, §3
- COMPANY will provide an interface management IT tool which CONTRACTOR must use — Exhibit A App A10, §3
