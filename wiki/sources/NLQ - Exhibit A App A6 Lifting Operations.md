## Summary

Appendix A6 (Rev A) defines the workshop/worksite/site lifting operations requirements for the NLQ EPCIC 10. It covers criticality classification, lifting plans, operator competencies, equipment inspection, and personnel basket rules. CONTRACTOR must conduct a gap analysis against COMPANY procedures at the preparation phase.

## Key Points

### General Organisation (§1)
- CONTRACTOR shall conduct a comprehensive gap analysis with COMPANY lifting procedures at early stage of preparation phase; gap analysis shall be submitted to COMPANY — Exhibit A App A6, §1
- CONTRACTOR's lifting policy, procedures, and lifting Risk Assessments shall be APPROVED by COMPANY — Exhibit A App A6, §1
- Lifting equipment at WORKSITES shall be certified by a recognised authority (refer to Exhibit J) — Exhibit A App A6, §1
- CONTRACTOR shall appoint a dedicated lifting superintendent with required certification, competence, and authority per SD-NOC-LH-001 — Exhibit A App A6, §1
- Lifting operations shall be compliant with NSL's International Lifting and Rigging Handbook, SD-NOC-LH-001, and CP-NOC-057 — Exhibit A App A6, §1

### Lifting at Contractor Worksite (§2)
- Requirements of SD-NOC-STR-411 shall apply — Exhibit A App A6, §2
- Criticality of all lifting operations shall be assessed using Lifting Operation Criticality Assessment table in Attachment 1 — Exhibit A App A6, §2
- CONTRACTOR shall issue within 60 days after Effective Date its list of critical lifting operations with required documentation level and approximate commencement dates — Exhibit A App A6, §2
- CONTRACTOR shall issue a Lifting Matrix addressing types of lifting, required documents, COMPANY notification timing, and control measures — Exhibit A App A6, §2

### Lifting at Company Site (§3)
- Requirements of SD-NOC-STR-401, SD-NOC-STR-404, SD-NOC-LH-001, and SD-NOC-LH-003 shall apply — Exhibit A App A6, §3
- All SITE lifting equipment and gears shall be certified by an external recognised accredited third party — Exhibit A App A6, §3
- Non-critical lifts: require non-critical lifting plan (Attachment 2), generic risk assessment, and pre-job meeting with crane operator — Exhibit A App A6, §3
- Critical lifts: require critical lifting plan (Attachment 3), specific risk assessment, kick-off meeting with all parties, and pre-job meeting — Exhibit A App A6, §3
- CONTRACTOR shall issue critical lifting operations list within 90 days after Effective Date; all lifting plans and risk assessments shall be issued at least 12 weeks prior to commencement for COMPANY review and APPROVAL — Exhibit A App A6, §3

### Competencies and Supervision (§4)
- CONTRACTOR shall identify a "Competent Person in Lifting Operation" (CPLO) within its organisation to act as reference for all lifting topics — Exhibit A App A6, §4
- CONTRACTOR CPLO or approved competent person shall be present during all lifting/movement operations to verify compliance with approved studies — Exhibit A App A6, §4
- Working in the danger zone is prohibited during all lifting activities — Exhibit A App A6, §4

### General Requirements (§5)
- All units shall be provided with inspected, certified, and colour-coded lifting points and attachment points — Exhibit A App A6, §5(i)
- Lifting devices for all units shall be provided including lifting subs, spreader bars, etc., suitable for both onshore and offshore, in air and submerged — Exhibit A App A6, §5(ii)
- All units subjected to sea transportation shall have designated facilities (pad eyes or similar) for sea fastening marked "Sea Fastening Only" — Exhibit A App A6, §5(iv)
- Rotating lifting points (swivels, threaded lift caps) shall not be used on units >50 kg; fully welded pad eyes required wherever possible — Exhibit A App A6, §5(viii)
- Use of hydraulic jack for lifting shall be considered a critical lift requiring method statement, calculations, and specific risk assessment APPROVED by COMPANY — Exhibit A App A6, §5(x)
- All lifting gears shall be tagged with: maximum weight capacity; reference number; validity date; colour code — Exhibit A App A6, §5(xiii)
- Lifting gear shall be certified by COMPANY-approved Third Party at mobilisation on WORKSITE and annually thereafter — Exhibit A App A6, §5(xiv)
- Home-made and self-assembled lifting devices are prohibited until CONTRACTOR provides evidence of standards compliance and COMPANY APPROVAL — Exhibit A App A6, §5(xvi)

### Inspection (§8)
- Maximum 6-month inspection interval for lifting equipment used to lift personnel and all lifting gear — Exhibit A App A6, §8(i)
- Maximum 12-month inspection interval for lifting appliances and other lifting equipment — Exhibit A App A6, §8(ii)
- Lifting gear shall be colour coded; new colour used after every thorough examination — Exhibit A App A6, §8
- When lifting gear is declared unsafe after inspection, it shall be destroyed and thrown away — Exhibit A App A6, §8

### Personnel Baskets (§10)
- Personnel basket lifting shall be avoided and considered last option when all other methods have been evaluated — Exhibit A App A6, §10
- Design shall comply with BS 14502-1, ASME B 30.23 or equivalent; inspection per BS 7121-2 (2012) — Exhibit A App A6, §10
- Crane used for man basket lifting must be marked/equipped suitable for man riding per API 2C or EN 13852 — Exhibit A App A6, §10

### Criticality Assessment (Attachment 1)
- 26-question criticality questionnaire determining CRITICAL classification; triggered by: >80% crane capacity, lifting personnel, live process area, 2-crane lifts, high monetary value, non-reversible operations, large sail area, subsea operations, etc. — Exhibit A App A6, Att. 1
