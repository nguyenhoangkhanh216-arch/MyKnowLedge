## Summary

CAPM/PMP exam reference sheets from Instructing.com covering the five main knowledge areas tested: Earned Value Management, Critical Path Method, quality tools, human resource motivation theories, risk management responses, and the 49 PMBOK processes across 5 process groups. Useful rapid-reference for PMP/CAPM exam preparation.

## Key Points

### Earned Value Management (EVM) Formulas

| Formula | Equation | Positive = | Negative = |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cost Variance (CV) | EV − AC | Under budget | Over budget |
| Schedule Variance (SV) | EV − PV | Ahead of schedule | Behind schedule |
| Cost Performance Index (CPI) | EV / AC | Under budget (>1) | Over budget (<1) |
| Schedule Performance Index (SPI) | EV / PV | Ahead of schedule (>1) | Behind schedule (<1) |
| EAC (standard) | BAC / CPI | — | — |
| EAC (future work at plan) | AC + BAC − EV | — | — |
| EAC (initial estimates flawed) | AC + Estimate for remainder | — | — |
| EAC (CPI × SPI affect) | AC + [BAC−EV / (CPI×SPI)] | — | — |
| ETC | EAC − AC | — | — |
| VAC | BAC − EAC | Under budget | Over budget |
| TCPI (using BAC) | (BAC−EV) / (BAC−AC) | Harder to meet BAC (>1) | Easier to meet BAC (<1) |

### Critical Path Method
- **Float** = LS − ES = LF − EF (same result)
- **Forward pass:** ES of first task = 1; EF = ES + duration − 1; next ES = previous EF + 1
- **Backward pass:** LF of last task = its EF; LS = LF − duration + 1; predecessor LF = successor LS − 1
- **Free float:** delay without affecting the NEXT activity's start
- **Total float:** delay without affecting the PROJECT end date

### Time Terms
- **Lag:** Waiting time between activities (positive)
- **Lead:** Activities overlap (negative)
- **Crashing:** Add resources → reduce duration → adds cost
- **Fast tracking:** Overlap phases → reduce duration → adds risk

### Task Relationships
- FS (Finish-to-Start): most common
- SS (Start-to-Start): tandem activities
- FF (Finish-to-Finish): both finish around same time
- SF (Start-to-Finish): rare, just-in-time scheduling

### Quality
- **Quality:** conformance to requirements; fitness for use
- **Grade:** category given to entities with same function but different technical characteristics
- **Gold plating:** adding extra features beyond scope — bad practice
- **Quality assurance:** prevention-driven (do it right first time)
- **Quality control:** inspection-driven (keep mistakes from customers)
- **Cost of poor quality:** rework, loss of life/limb, loss of sales
- **Rule of seven:** 7 consecutive data points on same side of mean = non-random / assignable cause

### Human Resource Theories
- **Maslow:** Physiological → Safety → Social → Esteem → Self-actualization
- **Herzberg:** Hygiene agents (expected: pay, safe environment) vs. Motivating agents (bonuses, advancement); hygiene absence demotivates but presence alone doesn't motivate
- **McGregor X/Y:** X = lazy/need micromanagement; Y = self-directed; most managers have both attributes
- **Ouchi Theory Z:** Workers do well if motivated; participative, familial, lifelong employment (Japanese style)
- **McClelland:** Needs = Achievement + Affiliation + Power (shaped by life experience)
- **Vroom Expectancy:** People behave based on expected outcome of their behavior

### PM Powers
- **Expert:** authority from experience
- **Reward:** authority to reward team
- **Formal/Positional:** assigned by senior management
- **Coercive/Penalty:** authority to discipline
- **Referent:** team personally knows PM

### Eight Risk Responses
**Threats:** Escalate, Avoid, Mitigate, Accept, Transfer
**Opportunities:** Escalate, Enhance, Exploit, Share

### Contracts
- **Cost reimbursable:** Risk with buyer (buyer pays cost overruns)
- **Fixed-price/Lump-sum:** Risk with seller (seller pays overruns)
- **Time & Materials:** Buyer pays time + materials; must have Not-To-Exceed (NTE) clause
- **Purchase Order:** Unilateral contract
- **Letter of Intent:** Not binding — buyer states intent

### 49 PMBOK Processes (process groups)
- **Initiating (2):** Develop Charter, Identify Stakeholders
- **Planning (24):** All plan processes across all knowledge areas
- **Executing (10):** Direct & Manage, Manage Quality, Acquire/Develop/Manage Resources, Manage Communications, Implement Risk Responses, Conduct Procurements, Manage Stakeholder Engagement
- **Monitoring & Controlling (12):** Monitor & Control Work, Perform Integrated Change Control, Validate/Control Scope, Control Schedule/Cost/Quality/Resources, Monitor Communications/Risks/Stakeholder Engagement, Control Procurements
- **Closing (1):** Close Project or Phase
