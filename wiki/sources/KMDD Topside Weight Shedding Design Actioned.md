## Summary

On 10 June 2026, Nguyễn Văn Tây officially directed all engineering teams to implement the weight shedding concept in design and procurement for DD-WHP topside. This moves the weight shedding from "study mode" to an official design change, triggered by the attached email from VSP/PMC confirming the concept. Teams must proceed with updating design and initiating corresponding procurement.

## Key Points

### Why Weight Shedding is Needed

- **WCR Rev.D1** (issued 23-May-2026): DD-WHP factored topside weight = **992.1 MT** (incl. rigging); KM-WHP = **930 MT**
- Crane vessel **Hoàng Sa** (VSP): lifting capacity = **900 MT** incl. rigging; max **600 MT per hook**
- Deficits: DD-WHP **+92.1 MT** over limit; KM-WHP **+30 MT** over limit
- DD-WHP is more critical and addressed first

### Approved Weight Shedding Concept (now official)

1. **Cantilever deck cut (Snapshot 1):** Separate a portion of the topside (Platform West, ~31.8 MT) as a separate lift piece to be installed offshore
2. **Vent boom:** Install vent boom as separate item offshore
3. **Solar panels / support structure:** Install solar panel and support on Platform West slab offshore; requires layout rearrangement (Solar Panel/Equipment moved East to avoid No-Go Zone from lifting paydeye; SAF escape route and Tote Tanks rearranged East)
4. **MEC item relocation:** During Load-Out (LO) and Transport & Installation (T&I), gather all MEC items on Main Deck and relocate to platform East to shift COG toward lifting point midpoint
5. **Counterweight:** Add 30 MT counterweight at platform East to shift COG

### Design Impact by Discipline

| Discipline | Impact |
|---|---|
| STR | Split topside into Part 1 + Part 2 (~31.8MT) + Vent Boom; cutline definition |
| ELE | Offshore HUC for Part 2 items (solar panels, equipment on Platform West) |
| PIP | No-Go Zone update post-shedding; hook-up routing offshore |
| MEC | All Main Deck MEC items shifted to platform East; COG calculation update |
| SAF | Escape route and Tote Tank rearrangement to East |

### Action (10 Jun 2026)
