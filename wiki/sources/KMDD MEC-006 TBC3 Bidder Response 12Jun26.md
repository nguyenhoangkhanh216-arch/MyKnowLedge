## Summary

Status report as of 12-Jun-2026: Only PVDT responded to TBC#3 Rev.C (covering layout and methanol pump integration). POS and DKE have not responded; POS requested extension to Monday 16-Jun. PVDT confirmed CI+MeOH combined skid is technically feasible with no lifting, CoG, structural, or vibration issues. An open motor power dispute emerged: PVDT proposes 0.09/0.18 kW DC motors for CI pumps, whereas Milton Roy datasheets indicate different values.

## Key Points

- **PVDT response received 12-Jun:**
  - Combined CI + Methanol Injection Skid on one skid: **confirmed technically feasible**
  - No issues with: lifting operations, centre of gravity (CoG), structural strength, or vibration from methanol pump
  - GAD (General Arrangement Drawing) of combined skid to be provided by Friday (13-Jun)
  - **Motor power dispute:** PVDT proposes 0.09 kW & 0.18 kW DC motors for CI pumps; PVDT treats Milton Roy datasheet calculations as reference only for DC motor selection — open issue requiring resolution
  - CI pump rated power confirmed: **0.25 kW** per PVDT
  - Methanol pump rated power confirmed: **7.5 kW Triplex** (unchanged)
  - IRCD standby power consumption: PVDT to clarify with IRCD vendor for ELE solar panel sizing

- **BCM#2 follow-up (08-Jun, from POS + DKE meetings):**
  - POS + DKE: both preliminarily confirmed combined skid feasible; need time for detailed layout, maintenance space, CoG, lifting assessment, and GAD
  - POS + DKE: acknowledged need to switch methanol pump from Simplex → Triplex; datasheet update with manufacturer required
  - Delivery time: **42 weeks DDP Purchaser's Warehouse** (both bidders); flexible if vendor data review accelerated

- **Deadline status (as of 12-Jun):**
  - PVDT: responded (TBC#3 Rev.C)
  - POS: no response; requested extension to Monday 16-Jun-2026
  - DKE: no response; expected same timeframe as POS
