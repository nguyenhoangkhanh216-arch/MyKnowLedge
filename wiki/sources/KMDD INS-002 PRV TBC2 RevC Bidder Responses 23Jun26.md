## Summary

On 23-Jun-2026, An Phu Technologies (Lam Van Vu, PM) submitted the final update to TBC#2 Rev.C for INS-002 Pressure Relief Valves. The submission included updated HIPPS PSV tag list (rev06) and Technical Srl company profile. KLE and Thanh An also submitted their TBC#2 Rev.C responses on 23-Jun. All 5 bidders have now completed TBC#2 Rev.C. The ferrite content dispute with Italian foundry Technical Srl (An Phu's sub-supplier) remains open and technically unresolved.

## Key Points

- **Package:** INS-002 Pressure Relief Valves
- **TBC round:** TBC#2 Rev.C (final round before TBE)
- **Submission date:** An Phu 23-Jun 07:48; KLE and Thanh An also 23-Jun; ATA 22-Jun; Hasse/TTE earlier
- **All 5 bidders now submitted:** Hasse/TTE (early), ATA (22-Jun), An Phu/KLE/Thanh An (23-Jun)
- **An Phu submission contents:**
  - Reply to TBC#2 Rev.C_APT 23 Jun 2026.XLS
  - Updated Tag List-01 for PSV with HIPPS PSV tags (rev06)
  - 25-3258 r6 ADD TAGS TEC.PDF — Technical Srl data sheet (revision 6)
  - Company Profile for Technical Srl.pdf
- **HIPPS PSV tags rev06** — reflects the scope reclassification (4 HIPPS PSVs added to base scope Jun 3)
- **Open ferrite content dispute (Technical Srl, Italian foundry):**
  - PTSC spec requires: 45–55% ferrite in DSS castings
  - Technical Srl standard range: 35–55% (per NORSOK M-630); narrowing to 45–55% described as "very unusual and narrow"
  - Metallurgical argument: 45–55% ferrite (more ferritic) conflicts with high toughness at low temperature — approximately 40–42% ferrite (more austenitic) gives better low-temperature impact properties
  - Additional dispute: PTSC requires metallography "of all components from each heat and lot" — foundry questions whether specimen metallography is sufficient
  - Raised by foundry on 11-Jun; still unresolved 23-Jun (12 days open)
  - Resolution requires: PTSC formal clarification or updated DTS; CPY endorsement for foundry to commit to PTSC spec
