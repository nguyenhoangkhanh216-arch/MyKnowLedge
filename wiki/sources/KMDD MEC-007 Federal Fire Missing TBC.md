## Summary

On 10 June 2026, Võ Anh Kiệt discovered that despite Federal Fire Engineering (FFE) responding to TBC#2 (on 5-Jun), PTSC has not received TBC#1 Rev.B response. Kiệt sent a reminder to FFE's PK Chai requesting submission by 11 June 2026.

## Key Points

- **Package:** MEC-007 — CO2 and Nitrogen Package
- **Bidder:** Federal Fire Engineering Pte Ltd (Singapore) — "Federal Fire" / FFE
- **Issue:** PTSC has **not received TBC#1 Rev.B** response from FFE
- **Action:** Võ Anh Kiệt (on behalf of Ms. Ngan) sent reminder 10-Jun; requested submission by **11-Jun-2026**
- **Confusing context:** FFE sent TBC#2 response to Ms. Ngan on 05-Jun (which included technical clarifications, CO2/N2 catalogue link, PCV freezing concern, and request for isometric drawing for discharge piping). However the prior TBC#1 Rev.B was apparently never formally submitted or received.

### TBC#1 Rev.B Requirements (as summarized after BCM)

- TBC#1 Rev.B submission
- Required documents: ITP, VDRL, Scope of Work, KTR, etc.
- Process calculation: number of CO2/N2 bottles for each skid (main + reserve for KM & DD), dimensions per skid
- Advise CO2 bottle concentration
- Calculation + Catalogue for Restriction Orifice (RO) to maintain required CO₂ discharge rate
- Calculation + Catalogue for PCV of Nitrogen Skids
- Method for identifying remaining gas quantity; criteria for recharging
- Relevant past projects

### Technical Issue Raised by FFE (05-Jun)

FFE raised a significant concern: a large pressure drop in the CO2 piping will cause **freezing of piping and PCV, resulting in choking**. FFE is evaluating RO plate or nozzle discharge options and requested isometric drawing from PTSC for the discharge piping from skid to vent stack/tip.

### Background

- MEC-007 previously flagged as critical: only TTE was "Acceptable" among 5 bidders in early evaluation; DKE, FFE, PPL, Mermaid all "Not Acceptable" — TBC#02 Rev.A issued Jun 3 to give additional bidders a chance
- FFE's commercial contact changed: Ms. Tran Thi Khanh Ngan (original point of contact) no longer in charge; Ms. Ngo Hoang Ngan now handles it
