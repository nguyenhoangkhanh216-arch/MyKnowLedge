## Summary
Exhibit N (Rev A) establishes the In-Country Value (ICV) obligations for the NLQ EPCIC 10 CONTRACTOR. ICV is QatarEnergy's TAWTEEN initiative to maximize in-Qatar economic benefit — local goods/services, workforce training, supplier development, and fixed asset investment. CONTRACTOR must prepare an ICV Plan, track it annually, and achieve at least 70% of the committed score to receive the ICV Provisional Sum.

## Key Points
- ICV Plan must be submitted at contract award; CONTRACTOR must comply with TAWTEEN "ICV Plan Template Guidelines" (downloadable at https://www.tawteen.com.qa). — Exhibit N, §4
- Four ICV components: (a) goods and services, (b) workforce training, (c) supplier development, (d) investment in fixed assets. — Exhibit N, §4.1
- If CONTRACT term > 1 year, CONTRACTOR completes an ICV Plan Tracker annually to monitor spend vs ICV Plan. — Exhibit N, §6.1
- 70% threshold: if CONTRACTOR achieves < 70% of last revised ICV Plan at ICV Evaluation Date, COMPANY will NOT release the ICV Provisional Sum (or sets CONTRACTOR's ICV Score to 0% for 6+ months on future tenders). — Exhibit N, §8.1
- ICV Provisional Sum is released pro-rata from 70% to 100% of ICV Plan performance; the Provisional Sum amount is fixed at award and does not increase with Change Orders. — Exhibit N, §§5.3, 9.1
- CONTRACTOR must hire a certified CERTIFIER (approved by QatarEnergy) at its own cost to perform ICV Certification at ICV Evaluation Date. — Exhibit N, §6.5
- If original ICV Plan is impacted (e.g., scope change), CONTRACTOR submits an ICV Impact Assessment Report within 1 month; COMPANY accepts/rejects at sole discretion; CONTRACTOR must continue performance regardless. — Exhibit N, §7
