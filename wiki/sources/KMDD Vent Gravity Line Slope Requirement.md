## Summary

Process Design Criteria specifies minimum slope 1:100 for gravity drain lines and vent lines. Piping team requested relaxation to 1:200 in specific areas (particularly the Pig trap area) due to layout constraints. Process team confirmed the 1:100 requirement remains in P&IDs, but acknowledged Piping should collect evidence of specific constraint areas and raise in multidiscipline engineering meeting with COMPANY.

## Key Points

- **Requirement per Process Design Criteria:** Minimum slope 1:100 for gravity drain lines and vent headers/lines
- **Piping team concern:** 1:100 is difficult to achieve in the Pig trap area because:
  - Pig trap height ~1200 mm above top of grating/plating
  - No adjustment room for fitting-to-fitting installation tolerance in the Z direction
  - Bottom flange (drain valve) to floor clearance inadequate (below FEED 150 mm minimum requirement)
- **Requested relaxation:** 1:200 slope in constrained areas
- **Process team response:** Will keep 1:100 in P&IDs per design criteria; Piping to collect evidence of specific constraint areas and raise in multidiscipline meeting with COMPANY for discussion
- **Status as of 2026-04-22:** Open — pending Piping team collecting evidence; not yet raised to COMPANY
