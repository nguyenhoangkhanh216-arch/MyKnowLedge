## Summary

PVEP-KM (Le Van Dong, Sr. Piping Engineer) formally accepted the KMDD firewater re-routing on 12-Jun-2026, closing the long-running stagnation concern about the GRE→CuNi material change. The thread spanned Apr–Jun 2026, with CPY initially demanding proof that stagnant sections were eliminated. PTSC responded by re-routing the ring main with proper slope and repositioning butterfly valves — CPY's final acceptance email adds two conditions going forward.

## Key Points

- **Stagnation concern status: CLOSED** — PVEP-KM accepted the re-routing design presented by PTSC (Trần Trọng Tín, Piping) on 10-Jun-2026
- **CPY conditions noted on 12-Jun:**
  1. Fabrication and installation of the firewater system must comply with design requirements to prevent stagnant water
  2. Pipe stress analysis must account for sagging of the piping system
- **Root cause of dispute:** PTSC issued the CuNi TQR without prior draft review with CPY Sr. Piping Engineer — CPY (Le Van Dong) escalated on 22-May requesting discussion before official issue; PTSC's process was to discuss with anh Hưng first (PVEP-KM management) which caused friction
- **Technical confirmation from PTSC Corrosion Team** (Nguyễn Thanh Nghĩa): CuNi 90/10 should not be kept stagnant in seawater for more than 9–12 months; intermittent service (as firewater) is acceptable per Norsok P-001 (up to 10 m/s)
- **Velocity check:** Process team confirmed that intermittent service velocity for CuNi is acceptable (not limited to 3 m/s — that applies to continuous service only)
- **Timeline of dispute:** PVEP-KM raised concern 25-May → PTSC presented routing 03-Jun → CPY requested short presentation 04-Jun → routing shown and re-routed per CPY request 10-Jun → accepted 12-Jun
