## Summary

This thread captures the full GRE → CuNi 90/10 material change for the KMDD firewater ring main, and CS Galvanized → SS 316 for fresh water. PTSC submitted a formal TQR (issued by corrosion team leader Nguyen Thanh Nghia) which was sent to VSP for review. CPY (PVEP-KM, Le Van Dong) raised a seawater stagnation concern regarding CuNi; PTSC responded that routing design already avoids stagnant sections. CPY's final email (04-Jun-2026) requested a short presentation confirming this routing design approach.

## Key Points

- **TQR officially issued** by PTSC corrosion team (Nguyen Thanh Nghia, Corrosion & Material Team Leader) and sent to VSP for review
- **CPY concern:** seawater stagnation inside CuNi firewater ring main — CuNi must not stagnate seawater >6–9 months (pinhole corrosion risk)
- **PTSC response (Vo Nhut Khanh, Piping):** routing design already accounts for stagnation prevention — design routing arranged to avoid stagnant sections. No stagnation expected.
- **CPY follow-up (04-Jun):** "Please set up a short presentation this afternoon to show how routing avoids stagnant sections. Next time, discuss with us before officially issuing the TQ."
- **Friction point:** PTSC issued the TQR without first sharing draft with CPY Sr. Piping Engineer (Le Van Dong); CPY is requiring this process discipline going forward
- **GRE vs CuNi technical comparison (submitted by Nguyen Thanh Nghia, Apr-2026):**

| Factor | GRE | CuNi 90/10 |
|---|---|---|
| Corrosion | UV degradation risk despite cert; ISO-21457 compliant | ISO-21457 compliant; intermittent service OK up to 10 m/s (Norsok P-001) |
| Stress/sizing | Lower allowable stress; larger pipe needed | Higher allowable → smaller pipe; compact design |
| Procurement | Specialized; single-source risk; long lead time | Widely available; established supply chain |
| Constructability | Costly training/certification for workers | Conventional welding; no special tools |
| Operation | Smooth internal surface | Must drain after use if stagnant >6 months |
| Maintenance | Complex repair (offshore vendor mobilization) | Easy weld repair; long service life |
| Weight | Light | +0.2 MT per platform |
| **Conclusion** | — | **CuNi preferred: lower procurement risk, higher mechanical strength, easier fabrication/repair** |

- **Fresh water (CS Galvanized → SS 316):** SS 316 cleaner, no zinc leaching, easier fabrication, weight reduction 0.6 MT/platform, preferred for eyewash/clean service
