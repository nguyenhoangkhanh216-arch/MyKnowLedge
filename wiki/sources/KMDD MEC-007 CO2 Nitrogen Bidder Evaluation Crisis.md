## Summary
Võ Anh Kiệt's internal assessment of MEC-007 bidder status (Jun 3) shows only 1 out of 5 bidders (TTE) is technically acceptable. On Jun 4 (21:15), Federal Fire Engineering (FFE) submitted Forms T1–T8 via email — 10 days past the 25-May deadline — but raised a critical technical concern: CO2 pressure drop through the PCV will cause line and valve freezing/choking. As of EOD Jun 4, TTE remains stale at TBC#1 RevC (7 days, no reply since 28-May).

## Key Points
- **Overall result: 1/5 bidders technically acceptable** — TTE only (as of Jun 3 evaluation).
- **DKE**: No response to TBC#1 Rev.A; missing Control Panel logic, bottle quantities, PCV sizing calc, bottle cert.
- **FFE (Federal Fire Engineering)**: BCM held 22-May; submitted Forms T1–T8 on **04-Jun-2026** (late, original deadline 25-May). Technical concern raised: *"Such a drastic drop in pressure will result in the freezing on the piping and PCC [PCV] and will result in choking."* Still requested isometric drawings for CO2 discharge piping to vent stack/tip to evaluate RO plate vs. nozzle discharge options.
- **PPL**: Missing KTR, experience list, CO2/N2 catalogue & drawing, PCV calc; no response to TBC#1 RevA; no bottle cert.
- **TTE**: Acceptable — stale on TBC#1 RevC since 28-May (7d as of EOD Jun 4); 9 rounds of exchange.
- **Mermaid**: No response to TBC#1 Rev.B; missing almost all docs; non-compliant with INS and PIP TBC.
- **Action taken Jun 3**: TBC#02 Rev.A sent to TTE and FFE.
- **CO2 PCV Freezing Issue (FFE, Jun 4)**: CO2 at near-ambient conditions undergoes Joule-Thomson cooling through a pressure regulator — risk of dry ice formation blocking the PCV and piping. FFE cannot resolve without knowing the piping isometrics from the skid to the vent tip.
