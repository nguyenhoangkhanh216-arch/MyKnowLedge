## Summary

PTSC formally responded on 23-Jun to An Phu Technologies / Technical Srl (Italian foundry) on the two open DSS (Duplex Stainless Steel) material specification disputes raised 11-Jun. PTSC accepted the ferrite range of 35–55% (down from the 45–55% in the original spec) and confirmed that metallography per heat batch (specimen of the heat) is sufficient. An Phu's foundry accepted NORSOK MDS D46 as the supply basis and submitted the document on 24-Jun.

## Key Points

- **Open dispute (raised 11-Jun, resolved 23-24 Jun — 13 days):**
  1. **Ferrite content:** PTSC original spec 45–55%; Italian foundry argued standard is 35–55% (NORSOK M-630) and that 45–55% conflicts with low-temperature toughness at -51°C → **PTSC accepted 35–55% as acceptable**
  2. **Metallography scope:** Foundry asked if specimen-of-heat is sufficient → **PTSC confirmed: each heat from the same heat treatment batch shall be tested; specimen per heat is acceptable**
- **Supply basis:** Technical Srl (Italian foundry) supplies DSS per **NORSOK MDS D46**; document submitted by An Phu on 24-Jun
- **Other DSS requirements confirmed (not disputed):** Max C 0.03%, min N 0.15%, CVN at -51°C (54J avg / 43J single), max hardness 285HV10 base / 320HV10 welded, min PREN >35 for 22%Cr, ASTM G48 Method A pitting test, 100% PMI, solution annealed + water quenched, pickled + passivated
