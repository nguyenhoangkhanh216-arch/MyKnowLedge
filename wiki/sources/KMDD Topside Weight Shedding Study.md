## Summary

WCR Rev. D1 (cut-off 20-May-2026) confirmed significant topside weight overruns for both KMDD WHPs versus the Hoàng Sa crane vessel's 900 MT lift capacity. DD-WHP has a 92.1 MT overrun and KM-WHP has a 30 MT overrun. A weight shedding study was initiated and presented on 08-Jun-2026 with four proposed measures. VSP/PMC decision is required to proceed — this is a study, not an implemented design change.

## Key Points

**Weight overrun confirmed (WCR Rev. D1, 20-May-2026):**
| Platform | Factored Weight (MT) | Crane Limit (MT) | Overrun (MT) |
|----------|---------------------|-----------------|--------------|
| **DD-WHP** | 992.1 | 900 | **+92.1 MT** |
| **KM-WHP** | 930 | 900 | **+30 MT** |

- Crane vessel: Hoàng Sa (PTSC's crane barge)
- Factored weight = unfactored weight × contingency factor
- PTSC STR team prepared weight analysis and shedding proposals

**Draft weight shedding measures (DD-WHP primary target):**

1. **Split topside into 2 parts** — Part 2 ≈ 31.8 MT + Vent Boom lifted separately offshore
2. **Relocate MEC items to Platform East** — Shift COG to balance against crane; reduce effective lift load
3. **Offshore HUC (Hook-Up and Commissioning) for Part 2 ELE items** — Remove ELE items from topsides lift weight; install offshore
4. **Add 30 MT counterweight at Platform East** — Structural counterbalance to achieve required lift geometry

**Key constraints:**
- Hoàng Sa crane: 900 MT maximum lift capacity
- Splitting the topside (Measure 1) is the most direct solution but significantly increases offshore HUC scope and cost
- Relocating MEC items (Measure 2) requires layout re-design
- All measures require VSP/PMC decision and formal FEED document revision

**Stakeholder note (Nguyen Van Tay, PTSC):**
*"This is a study to evaluate options; the base case model proceeds as before"*
— The shedding measures are not yet approved; the 3D model and ongoing detailed engineering continue on the original base case until VSP/CPY makes a formal decision.

**Meeting and escalation:**
- Weight shedding presentation slides prepared by STR team
- Meeting with VSP scheduled for afternoon of 08-Jun-2026
- All disciplines asked to report their weight impact by morning 08-Jun
- VSP PM (senior level) involved — weight overrun is a project-critical issue

**Context — why this matters:**
- The WCR Rev. D1 represents the first formal weight report post-FEED
- 992.1 MT vs 900 MT for DD-WHP means the platform as currently designed CANNOT be installed by Hoàng Sa
- Either the topside must be lightened, split, or a different crane vessel must be sourced
- This is a major project-level decision with schedule and commercial implications
