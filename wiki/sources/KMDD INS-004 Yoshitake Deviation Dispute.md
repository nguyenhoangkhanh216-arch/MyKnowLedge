## Summary
Yoshitake responded to the INS-004 TBC stating that their manufacturer cannot fully comply with project requirements for pressure regulators and that they will be unable to proceed if all deviations must be confirmed compliant. This creates a significant bidder availability risk for the package, which already had limited responders.

## Key Points
- **Yoshitake's position (Jun 3)**: "Before we quoted, we were informed deviations could be accepted. Based on the TBC, you now want confirmation of full compliance — this is not possible. If deviations cannot be accepted, we cannot proceed."
- **Root cause**: Apparent miscommunication — verbal agreement to accept deviations not reflected in the formal TBC requirements, which asks for compliance confirmation on all points.
- **Yoshitake's earlier stance (Apr 28)**: Initially tried to withdraw entirely, citing manufacturer specification non-compliance and potential CA requirement issues.
- **PTSC response at the time**: Clarified CA is not mandatory; persuaded Yoshitake to re-engage.
- **Current risk**: Yoshitake may still drop out if PTSC does not formally accept deviations in writing.
- **Other bidders for INS-004**: HAPCO (responded, cooperative), KVA (requested extension to Jun 5), TTE (awaiting), MOTEC (awaiting). Package has 5 bidders in TBC.
- **Action needed**: PTSC must decide whether to formally accept Yoshitake's deviations or allow their exit.
