## Summary

Exhibit H Rev A (30 pages) defines the complete Quality Assurance and Quality Control framework for the NLQ EPCIC 10 contract at Al Shaheen Oil Field, Qatar. It covers CONTRACTOR QMS structure, project quality plan, quality control, NCR/deviation procedures, subcontractor/vendor surveillance, COMPANY audit rights, quality organisation requirements, and final quality dossier.

## Key Points

### QMS Structure — Two Components
- CONTRACTOR must operate two QMS components: (1) a perennial company QMS certified to ISO 9001:2015 and (2) a contract-specific QMS documented in a Project Quality Plan (PQP) — Exhibit H, §3
- The PQP relies on two key documents: the Project Quality Plan and the Quality Control Plan(s) (also called Inspection and Test Plans) — Exhibit H, §3.2
- ISO 29001:2020 is accepted as an alternative to ISO 9001:2015 for oil and gas contractors — Exhibit H, §3.1

### Project Quality Plan (PQP) — Submission Timeline
- Offer stage: preliminary PQP, Quality Manual, ISO 9001 certificate, control of documents procedure, internal audit procedure, contract review evidence — Exhibit H, §4.2.1
- Within 30 days of EFFECTIVE DATE: intermediate PQP version + preliminary Quality Control Plan(s) submitted for COMPANY review — Exhibit H, §4.2.2
- Within 60 days of EFFECTIVE DATE: final PQP + first Quality Control Plan(s) with COMPANY surveillance points submitted for COMPANY APPROVAL — Exhibit H, §4.2.3
- PQP must be reviewed and revised at minimum once per year; all modifications require COMPANY APPROVAL before implementation — Exhibit H, §4.2.4

### Quality KPIs — Mandatory Targets
- Close-out of Corrective Action Requests from COMPANY audits: within 3 months — Exhibit H, §4.1
- NCR closure rate: 90% within 60 days — Exhibit H, §4.1
- Punch list closure rate: 95% at Sail Away — Exhibit H, §4.1
- Weekly rolling 4-week reporting of: welding repairs, structural/piping/pipeline welder rejection rates, NDT backlogs — Exhibit H, §4.1
- KPIs reported at weekly QAQC Meetings and in Monthly Report; reviewed at 6-monthly Project Management Review — Exhibit H, §4.1

### Technical Query (TQR) and Deviation Request (DEV) Process
- TQRs for clarification of COMPANY requirements: submitted via agreed TQR procedure + dedicated form + register; must be signed by CONTRACTOR REPRESENTATIVE — Exhibit H, §4.3.5
- DEV for requests to deviate from COMPANY requirements: must be issued PRIOR to implementing any deviation — Exhibit H, §4.3.5
- DEV returned with Code 2 (Approved With Conditions): CONTRACTOR must resubmit with evidence that COMPANY comments implemented — Exhibit H, §4.3.5
- DEVs must also be signed by CONTRACTOR Quality Representative before submission — Exhibit H, §4.3.5
- Subcontractors/vendors submit TQRs and DEVs through CONTRACTOR, not directly to COMPANY — Exhibit H, §4.3.5

### NCR and Non-Conformance Procedure
- NCR procedure: documented; NCR form and register required; major non-conformities submitted to COMPANY for APPROVAL — Exhibit H, §4.3.10
- CONTRACTOR must respond within 5 days to any NCR (from COMPANY, CONTRACTOR, or vendor) stating prevention/remedial work and timeframe — Exhibit H, §4.3.10
- Non-conforming items: quarantined and identified to prevent use until NCR closed and APPROVED — Exhibit H, §4.3.10
- Concession Request (for accepting non-conforming products): separate form and register; requires COMPANY acceptance before incorporation; must be signed by CONTRACTOR Quality Representative — Exhibit H, §4.3.10
- Quality Event Report (QER): simplified reporting for minor site defects; QER register maintained by CONTRACTOR; escalates to formal NCR if no improvement — Exhibit H, §4.3.10
- NCRs and Concession Requests submitted through EDMS - MANASA — Exhibit H, §4.3.10

### CRA Materials — Specific Quality Requirements (Al Shaheen)
- CRA materials (SS, DSS, SDSS, 6Mo, Nickel, Titanium, Copper-Alloys) must be stored, cut, bent, welded, inspected, and tested in a dedicated workshop physically separated from any ferrite contamination source — Exhibit H, §4.3.3 and §5.12.1
- Positive Material Identification (PMI): 100% PMI on all CRA components and welds during prefabrication and erection; also required during pre-commissioning as-installed inspection — Exhibit H, §5.12.1
- PMI procedure submitted to COMPANY for APPROVAL; PMI inspection frequency documented in Quality Control Plan — Exhibit H, §5.12.1
- Material substitution for CRA: processed via DEV procedure with CSCC (Chloride Stress Corrosion Cracking) technical justification — Exhibit H, §5.12.1
- CRA coating: per SD-NOC-COR-350 — Exhibit H, §5.12.1

### Ex Equipment — Third-Party Audit Requirement
- During construction, CONTRACTOR must mobilise a third-party (subject to COMPANY APPROVAL) to audit Ex Equipment installation for compliance with IEC 60079-14 — Exhibit H, §5.12.2
- This TPI audit does not replace CONTRACTOR's own inspection scope — Exhibit H, §5.12.2

### Quality Control Plans — Structure and Surveillance Points
- Four categories of Quality Surveillance: Review (R), Monitoring (M), Witness Point (W), Hold Point (H) — Exhibit H, §5.3
- Hold Point (H): COMPANY must be notified; execution suspended if COMPANY absent — Exhibit H, §5.3
- Design review, FMA studies, HAZOP, and HAZID are classified as Hold points — Exhibit H, §7.2
- Inspection notification timelines: 15 days for KOM/PIM; 15 days for foreign country inspection; 7 days for in-country; 2 working days at WORKSITES — Exhibit H, §5.8
- 6-week look-ahead inspection/surveillance programme required — Exhibit H, §5.8
- Inspection reports issued within 2 working days of inspection; via NOC ShareFile platform — Exhibit H, §5.9

### Subcontractor and Vendor Quality Requirements
- Pre-qualification dossier required for any new vendor outside APPROVED list; includes ISO 9001 certificate, QMS, HSE data, financial data, anti-corruption policy — Exhibit H, §6.1
- Criticality rating 1 or 2 items: pre-inspection meeting mandatory; COMPANY entitled to attend — Exhibit H, §6.3
- New/recently approved subcontractors: surveillance at criticality rating 1 level — Exhibit H, §6.3
- All materials/equipment not accompanied by correct documentation and identification (tagging) on arrival at WORKSHOP: quarantined as non-conforming — Exhibit H, §6.4 and §6.5

### Quality Organisation Requirements
- Project Quality Manager: key personnel per Exhibit K; COMPANY APPROVAL required; CV and mobilisation/demobilisation plan submitted — Exhibit H, §9.1
- Audit technical expert qualifications: NDT Level III, AWS/IIW Welding Engineer, FROSIO/NACE Level III (coating), CompEx (electrical) — each with 10 years minimum experience — Exhibit H, §4.3.14
- Process audits mandatory for: NDT, Welding, Coating, GRP/GRE, Electrical installation, Flange Management — minimum 1 auditor + 1 technical expert full-time at each CONTRACTOR/SUBCONTRACTOR site — Exhibit H, §4.3.14
- Minimum 1 QMS audit covering entire scope every 6 months — Exhibit H, §4.3.14
- Piping/Flange personnel: trained in piping cleanliness, flange fit-up, bolt torquing/tensioning; recorded in dedicated register with unique ID and hard hat sticker — Exhibit H, §9.3.2
- Ex Equipment inspectors: trained/certified per IEC 60079; recorded in register with unique ID — Exhibit H, §9.3.3

### Quality Dossier — Final Documentation
- Quality Dossier (Volume 2) compiled from ED through CONTRACT completion; includes 61 document types per Table 10-1 — Exhibit H, §10
- Vendor Final Dossier delivered no later than 1 month after Factory Acceptance Test (FAT) — Exhibit H, §10.1
- First revision of Quality Record Book index submitted to COMPANY within 6 weeks after EFFECTIVE DATE — Exhibit H, §10.2
- Material traceability: structure drawings marked up with weld identification and material certificate numbers — Exhibit H, §10.1

### Certification — Governed by Appendix H1
- Certifying Authority (CA) contracted by CONTRACTOR as ancillary service; must be different from CONTRACTOR's second-party inspection agency — Exhibit H, §8
- Safety and lifting equipment (including HIPPS): CA at all phases (E/P/I/C) — Exhibit H App H1, §6
- Main bearing structure: CA for E/P/I; QC only for C — Exhibit H App H1, §6
- Critical production equipment: CA for E/P; QC for I and C — Exhibit H App H1, §6
