Our legal team have completed the review of the Subcontract (IFR) submitted and, in the spirit of establishing a mutually balanced and workable agreement, we would like to propose the following clarifications and amendments for your consideration:

 

* * *

**1\. Limitation of Liability (Article 19)**

While we note that the liability cap is stated as 100% of the Subcontract Value, the current wording includes several exclusions (e.g. Articles 6, 15, 18, 21) which effectively render the cap inapplicable in a number of scenarios.

**Proposal:**  
We kindly request that the limitation of liability applies **on an aggregate basis to all liabilities arising under the Subcontract** , including indemnities, except for customary carve-outs such as fraud and wilful misconduct.

 

PTSC M&C response: Agreed that liabilities for personal injury and property damage under Article 6 (Indemnity) are included within the overall Limitation of Liability (LOL). However, liabilities relating to patents, intellectual property rights (IPR), confidentiality, and compliance with law are standard exclusions and therefore should not be included within the LOL. **Noted and agreed**

 

 

* * *

**2\. Indemnity (Article 6)**

The indemnity provisions are currently broad and may expose the Subcontractor (TUV Rheinland Risktec) to liabilities beyond its reasonable control, including third-party claims.

**Proposal:**  
We propose that:

  * Indemnities be limited to the extent of **Subcontractor’s proven negligence** , and
  * All indemnity obligations be made **subject to the overall limitation of liability** under Article 19.



 

 

**3\. Consequential Loss (Article 6.4)**

The current clause introduces potential liability for consequential losses (e.g. loss of production, revenue, etc.), which is not typical for consultancy services.

**Proposal:**  
We recommend inclusion of a **mutual waiver of consequential and indirect losses** , regardless of cause, except in cases of fraud or wilful misconduct.

 

PTSC M&C response: We have provided our comments as set out in the attached file. 

For Article 6.4, we agree with the concept that consequential loss includes both consequential and indirect losses; however, this shall apply only as between the CONTRACTOR and the SUBCONTRACTOR.

This represents our team’s best position on the above matters.  **Noted and agreed**

* * *

**4\. Payment Terms (Article 3)**

We note the “pay-when-paid” provision of CONTRACTOR by COMPANY
