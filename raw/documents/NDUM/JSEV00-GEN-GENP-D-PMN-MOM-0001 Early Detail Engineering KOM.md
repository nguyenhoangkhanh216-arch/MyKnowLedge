5 – Early Detail Engineering – T&I
Early engagement of the T&I Subcontractor is critical to minimize execution risk, avoid late design changes,
and ensure project schedule certainty. Key high-impact areas requiring early T&I involvement include:
1. Finalization of HLV / Marine Spread Strategy
✓ Confirm suitability and availability of HLV in the market (EOI of T&I Sub, potential HLVs/ DSV…).
✓ Finalize installation philosophy (jacket’s lifting vs. launching, requirement of buoyancy tanks…).
✓ Avoid major redesign caused by vessel limitation or market availability constraints.
2. Hot Tapping Tie-in Concept to existing pipeline
✓ Develop and mature the hot tapping and tie-in strategy to existing pipelines.
✓ Define specialized equipment needs such as double block and bleed (DBB) valve, hot tap machine skid…
✓ Minimize execution risk and avoid schedule impact due to late interface alignment
3. Long-Lead Material Definition
✓ Early confirmation of critical MTO (structural steels, flanges, subsea valves, hot tapping services).
✓ Ensure timely procurement of long-lead items and fabrication readiness.
✓ Minimize schedule impact due to material shortage or late delivery
