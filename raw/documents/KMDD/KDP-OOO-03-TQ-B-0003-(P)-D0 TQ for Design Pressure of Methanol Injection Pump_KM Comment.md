QUERY DESCRIPTION:  
As current design, the Methanol Injection System on KM-WHP is provided to inject methanol into upstream of choke to 
prevent hydrate formation downstream of choke valve during well black start & restart up, hence the Methanol Injection 
Pump discharge pressure is calculated based on the maximum destination pressure, which is the maximum CITHP of all 
wells as specified in the KM-WHP Process and Utilities Design Basis [Ref. 1], plus total pressure losses through the 
injection quill, tubing, fittings, flow element, and associated piping components. 
During detail design, the maximum CITHP for KM-WHP wells was increased from 184.7 barg to 186.6 barg, resulting in 
the required discharge pressure increasing from 193.34 barg to 195.4 barg, respectively. Refer to Attachment 1 for detailed 
Methanol Injection Pump calculation based on latest hydraulic calculation .

As per Section 9.4.3 KM-WHP Process & Utilities Design Basis [Ref. 1] requirement for positive displacement pump 
discharge systems: 
• 
Design Pressure = MOP + 20% for MOP > 10 barg 
Accordingly, the required design pressure for the Methanol Injection Pump discharge system is calculated as follow: 
• 
195.4 × 1.2 = 234.5 barg 
However, as specified in Project Piping Material Specification [Ref. 3], for piping class 153410X – SS316, the maximum 
allowable design pressure at 65°C is limited to 232 barg. The calculated required design pressure exceeds the piping class 
allowable pressure by 2.5 bar which may require an upgrade for piping rating. Keeping current design pressure of 232 barg 
will result in a margin of only 18.73% from operating pressure compared to requirement of 20%. 
 
PROPOSED SOLUTION:  
It should be noted that the Methanol Injection Pump (P-5345) is intended for intermittent operation only during cold start-
up to prevent hydrate formation downstream of the wellhead choke valve. Morever, there is PAH/PZAHH and a PSV at 
pump discharge, set at 232 barg and sizing for block outlet scenario, to protect the system from overpressure. Therefore, 
reducing the pressure design margin from 20% to 18.73% should not be a concern. 
Based on findings and justification above, SUB-CONTRACTOR proposes to maintain design pressure of KM-WHP 
Methanol Injection Pump (P-5345) at 232 barg in accordance with current class 153410X limitation in order to avoid 
unnecessary changes and be consistent with DD-WHP. No piping class upgrade will be required for the Methanol Injection 
System. 
Hence, by this TQ, SUB-CONTRACTOR kindly requests COMPANY to review and approve the above proposal for our 
further engineering and procurement execution.
