INTRODUCTION 
1.1 
PROJECT BACKGROUND 
Murphy Cuu Long Bac Oil Co Ltd. (Murphy Oil) is the Operator for Block 15-1/05 located in the 
East Sea approximately 120km due East of the Vung Tau offshore supply base in Vietnam. Water 
depth varies across the Block from 45 to 50 meters.

1.2 
OBJECTIVES 
Block 15-1/05 is being developed in phases with phase 1A composes of two parts: the LDV-A 
Central Production facilities and associated pipelines along with the LDV-B WHP installation 
post-production of the LDV-A central facilities. LDV-A First Oil is targeted Q4 2026. LDV-B is 
planned to be brought online 20 to 24 months after LDV-A and subject to the performance of 
LDV-A. The objectives of this scope of work are to: 
- 
Identify remaining engineering work necessary to enable the Tender Documentation to be 
compiled, such that full LDV-B Lumpsum turnkey EPCI contract strategy can be adopted. 
CONTRACTOR shall provide a set of CTRs for all deliverables identified.  
- 
Provide an updated Cost Estimate with risk profiles and benchmarking to enable the 
COMPANY to assess the validity of current estimates.  
- 
Prepare a draft ITB for a EPCIC for LDV-B from the existing LDV-A ITB/unpriced contract 
modified to LDV-B design and the EPCIC Lumpsum nature. 
- 
Support COMPANY in preparation and approvals of overall contracting strategies for LDV-B. 
Note: LDV-B pipelines are addressed in this scope of work but the option to bid out separately 
has been retained.

2.2 
UNITS AND CONVENTIONS 
2.2.1 Units 
The International System of Units (SI) should be used in all design, engineering documents and 
drawings, unless otherwise specified.  
The following exceptions are noted: 
• 
Standard gas flow will be specified in MMscfd (million standard cubic feet per day) with 
standard conditions defined as 1.01325 Bara and 15 DegC; 
• 
Standard liquid volume flow will be specified in stock tank BPD (standard barrels per day) 
with standard conditions defined as 1.01325 Bara and 15.6 DegC; and 
• 
Pressure will be specified in Barg. 
2.2.2 Convention 
The following convention will apply: 
• 
Wave and wind directions are expressed as the direction “FROM” which the wind and wave 
are approaching and in nautical degrees, i.e. degrees relative to True North (*T) and (+ve) 
clockwise; 
• 
Current direction is expressed as the direction “TOWARDS”, which the current is flowing in 
accordance with the oceanographic convention. Similarly, the current directions are relative 
to True North (*T).
