1 SCOPE OF WORK
In furtherance of Article 24 of the Agreement, this EXHIBIT L describes the detailed Health, Safety, Social
(local community), Security and Environment (HSE) requirements to be applied by CONTRACTOR during
all phases of WORK.
Unless otherwise expressly sets out in the CONTRACT, CONTRACTOR shall bear the cost and any and all
impacts on the WORK TIME SCHEDULE (WTS) resulting from, or otherwise connected with, any of the
following actions:
i. Meet the specified HSE regulations and other safety-related requirements.
ii. Perform the WORK in accordance with industry best practice in the oil and gas industry
and established available industry practices for environmental performance.
iii. Meet all HSE requirements.
iv. Measures and actions taken by COMPANY in lieu of CONTRACTOR on the grounds of
CONTRACTOR failing to take the necessary HSE measures and actions.
2 DEFINITIONS
The following expressions and derivatives thereof, appearing in capital letters in this EXHIBIT, shall have
the meaning hereby assigned to them unless otherwise specified, it being understood that such
expressions appearing in small letters shall have their common meaning as the context requires.
- CHEMICALS MANAGEMENT PLAN (CMP) means the plan which details the operational procedures
specifying the environmental criteria for selection of chemicals based on the sensitivity of the
surrounding environment and establishes the inventory of the chemicals used. It details the
toxicological and eco toxicological data, provided by the corresponding Material Safety Data Sheets
(MSDS) and defines the procedures for their handling, storage and disposal.
- COMPANY EMERGENCY RESPONSE PLAN means the plan setting out details of the series of
emergency measures provided by COMPANY, including the COMPANY’s Contingency Plan (i.e.
emergency measures provided at WORKSITE) and a specific intervention plan (i.e. series of
emergency measures provided in response to a specific type of incident or to regulatory
requirements).
- COMPANY HEALTH PLAN means the permanent document establishing the appropriate strategy,
means and conditions of medical and health support applicable during COMPANY’s activities. It
stipulates the PERSONNEL, equipment and procedures required for this support.
- COMPANY SECURITY PLAN (CSP) means the plan specifying the policies, standards, procedures
and responsibilities in order to provide guidance in the process of managing security and defining
the overall security aspects inside a perimeter.
- CONTRACTOR EMERGENCY RESPONSE PLAN represents a series of emergencies procedures or
measures prepared by CONTRACTOR to provide response to emergencies likely to occur during the
performance of SERVICES. This plan shall be compatible with COMPANY’s EMERGENCY RESPONSE
PLAN as may be complemented by the HSE BRIDGING DOCUMENT.
- CONTRACTOR HSE PLAN means the plan setting out details of a series of documents defining the
procedures and measures to implement all HSE requirements under the CONTRACT.
- ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT PLAN (EMP) means the part of the ENVIRONMENTAL
MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (EMS) which includes the operational procedures to ensure the control of
activities with potential impact on the environment. It includes the WASTE MANAGEMENT PLAN,
CHEMICALS MANAGEMENT PLAN, effluent treatments, noise reduction, protection measures and
monitoring procedures.

16.4.1 The Voluntary Principles on Security and Human Rights
16.4.1.1 The Voluntary Principles on Security and Human Rights (the Voluntary Principles) are a set of non-
binding principles agreed in dialogue between a group of governments, a number of companies in the
extractive and energy sectors and several non-governmental organisations. Although voluntary, they
attempt to guide companies faced with the challenge of maintaining the safety and security of their
operations whilst simultaneously respecting and promoting human rights.
16.4.1.2 The Voluntary Principles lay down guidelines in relation to risk assessment, relations between
companies and government security forces, and relations between companies and private security
providers. They are based around three (3) central concepts:
i. The necessity of a comprehensive risk assessment for informing a company’s operations
and its ability to mitigate the risk of human rights violations;
ii. The importance of communication, consultation and transparency, particularly with
regard to relationships with local communities; and,
iii. The importance of monitoring the conduct of governmental security forces (particularly
as regards proportionality in the use of force), their use of equipment provided by
companies, and their (and their government’s) response to alleged violations of human
rights. The potential for companies to use their influence to promote human rights
compliant activities in these areas is also underlined.
16.4.2 The United Nations (UN) Basic Principles on Use of Force and Firearms by Law Enforcement Officials
The UN Basic Principles on the Use of Force and Firearms by Law Enforcement Officials (the Basic
Principles) provide guidance to governments and law enforcement officials as to the use of force and
firearms. Referred to in the Voluntary Principles, they emphasise, as the Voluntary Principles do, that
force should be used as a last resort, and when used, should be used in a restrained and proportionate
manner to minimise injury. The Basic Principles also emphasise the importance of giving assistance and
medical aid to those injured by the use of force, and the necessity of notifying both the relevant
authorities and friends and family of the status of such persons.
16.4.3 The United Nations (UN) Code of Conduct for Law Enforcement Officials
The UN Code of Conduct for Law Enforcement Officials (the Code of Conduct) provides guidance as to the
proper conduct of law enforcement officials. Also referred to in the Voluntary Principles, it emphasises
that law enforcement officials should maintain and uphold the human rights of all persons, in particular
in relation to the prohibition on torture and inhuman and degrading treatment. Similarly to both the
Voluntary Principles and the Basic Principles, it stresses the importance of force being used as a last resort
and then only in a proportionate manner, and that the use of firearms is to be considered an extreme
measure. Finally, the code of conduct also promotes the abolition of corrupt practices among law
enforcement officials.
