1 PURPOSE
This Appendix describes the Conformity Assessment activities which shall be performed, as a minimum, by
CONTRACTOR appointed Third Party/Independent Verification Body (Basic Certification or Certifying Authority)
during the execution of the WORK.
2 GENERAL
Basic Certification or Certifying Authority shall provide evidence that all CONTRACT and SPECIFICATION
requirements have been met by CONTRACTOR. CONTRACTOR shall issue the certificates required by
SPECIFICATION, or applicable regulations.
Where Certifying Authority Scope of WORK is duplicated with the one of the Marine Warranty Surveyor, the
duplicated services shall be performed by both the Certifying Authority and Marine Warranty Surveyor (MWS).
CONTRACTOR shall nominate within their organisation a responsible for the certification dossier.
Any general or specific requirements related to the scope of the Certifying Authority or more generally to the
scope of a Third Party, as defined in COMPANY’s Standards (SD), or other documents listed in EXHIBIT E, shall be
included in CONTRACTOR Scope of WORK.
3 DEFINITIONS
For purposes of this document, the following terms are applicable and consistent with the ISO/IEC 17000 and
ISO/IEC 17020 standards.
Accreditation: Attestation by which an authoritative body conveys formal demonstration that a certification
body is competent to carry-out conformity assessment tasks.
Accredited Means: Accredited by a member of the European Cooperation for Accreditation (EA) or of the
International Accreditation Forum (IAF).
Basic Certification: The Basic Certification shall be contracted by CONTRACTOR as an independent service and
be part of the scope of WORK as an ancillary service through the CONTRACT as described in EXHIBIT A. The
certification is given by a Non-accredited Third Party which shall be APPROVED. The certification scope of WORK
shall be also APPROVED. The Basic Certification company shall be selected from the list of APPROVED
SUBCONTRACTORS/VENDORS LIST (Refer to EXHIBIT J, when applicable).
Certificate of Conformity: Document issued for the PLANT stating Conformity for the complete installations
including design, fabrication, installation hook-up and commissioning and final testing. The document is issued
at the completion of the specified activities.
Certification: Process by which an independent Third Party gives written assurance that a product, process or
service conforms to specified requirements.
Certifying Authority (CA): The accredited Certifying Authority shall be contracted by CONTRACTOR as an
independent Third Party and be part of the scope of WORK as an ancillary service through the CONTRACT, as
described in EXHIBIT A and EXHIBIT H. Third Party CA certification is given by an Accredited Third Party. The
Certifying Authority company shall be selected from the list of APPROVED SUBCONTRACTORS/VENDORS LIST
(Refer to EXHIBIT J, when applicable).
Conformity Assessment: All activities (Certification, Quality Surveillance and Quality Control) to assess the
conformity of the WORK.
First Party: Person or organisation that provides the object. Enterprise under contract with a COMPANY entity
to perform a service or deliver an object.
Inspection: Examination of a product design, product process or installation and determination of its conformity
with specific requirements or, on the basis of professional judgment, with general requirements.
Marine Warranty Surveyor: The Marine Warranty Surveyor (MWS) is a Third Party contractor recognised by
insurance companies. An MWS is not a certification body as such; it acts on behalf of the insurance companies
to reduce the operational risks that are covered. The Marine Warranty Surveyor is contracted by COMPANY.

Quality Control: is CONTRACTOR’s operational processes to ensure that designed, procured, manufactured and
installed facilities comply with CONTRACT requirements. Quality Control includes all technical observation
(inspection and testing) used to fulfill predefined requirements for quality, as well as all associated records. QC
is performed by CONTRACTOR.
Quality Surveillance (not referring to ISO 9000): Quality Surveillance is the customer process of monitoring that
Quality Assurance and Quality Control activities are properly implemented within the supply chain. Quality
Surveillance applies to COMPANY’s surveillance of CONTRACTOR activities. Quality Surveillance also refers to
CONTRACTOR’s surveillance of SUBCONTRACTORS and VENDORS.
Note: ‘Supply Chain’ is to be understood in its larger sense, from a COMPANY or CONTRACTOR point of view,
even if it is internally performed. It should cover all the WORK. It comprises document reviews, auditing,
inspection, and testing activities, as required.
Registration or Classification: Registration is the procedure by which a Third Party body indicates relevant
characteristics of a product, process or service in an appropriate, publicly available list. The Registration
Company shall be selected from the list of providers who are all members of the IACS (Refer to EXHIBIT J, when
applicable). CONTRACTOR shall confirm the validity of the classification society registration certificate just before
the request for classification of the concerned WORK. In case of invalid certificate at that time, COMPANY shall
immediately be informed of such, and a joint action with COMPANY shall be initiated to, if necessary, appoint
another provider from the list. Where one of the Third Party listed is nominated for classification, the
classification scope of work shall be taken as the main basis.
Second Party: Person or organisation that has a user interest in the object; e.g. in case of VENDOR, would mean
CONTRACTOR or COMPANY.
Statement of Conformity: A statement confirming that specified requirements are met. Statement of
Conformity shall be included in all certification deliverables.
Survey: The combination of spot checks, witness testing, inspection, surveillance of inspection done by Certifying
Authority Representative, scrutiny of inspection and maintenance records and any other relevant
documentation or statement for the purpose of giving a status of the installation or parts thereof in connection
with the certification.
Survey Report: Document issued by Certifying Authority, giving status of interventions carried out. Survey.
Third Party: Person or body that is independent of the person or organisation that provides the object, and of
user interests in that object. Note: An inspection company acting on a COMPANY entity detailed instruction is
not a third party, but a second party.

7 REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
In addition to Clause 6, CONTRACTOR shall identify and comply with Regulatory Compliance such as permits,
licenses, approvals, notifications and associated laws and regulations that are relevant to the SCOPE OF WORK,
in particular with Qatar Decree Law Nº 4 of 1977 on Preserving Petroleum Resources.
CONTRACTOR shall prepare a PLANT Certification Scope of Work in conjunction with the Certifying Authority in
order to comply with the Qatar Decree Law Nº 4 of 1977 on Preserving Petroleum Resources.
Certifying Authority shall issue to all PARTIES when the activity is completed the following certificates:
 Certificate of Conformity for the PLANT Design.
 Certificate of Conformity for the PLANT Procurement, Fabrication and Construction Survey.
 Certificate of Conformity for the PLANT Installation Survey.
 Certificate of Conformity for the overall PLANT.
Above certificates of conformity shall be issued by the Certifying Authority branch based in Qatar.
PLANT Certification monthly meeting shall be organised by CONTRACTOR with the presence of the Certifying
Authority and COMPANY.
8 REPORTING
Throughout the period for which the here-above Certifying requirements are applicable, the Certifying Authority
shall issue to CONTRACTOR and COMPANY monthly reports describing:
 Progress made in the Conformity Assessment activities.
 Outstanding matters.
 Problems arising from above.
9 CERTIFICATION DOSSIER
CONTRACTOR shall be responsible to maintain continuously updated a compiled dossier of all the Third Party(ies)
records (e.g. Recommendations, Inspection reports, Interim/Provisional/Final Acceptance Certificates,
Certificate of Conformity, Statement of Conformity, Survey Report). At any time during CONTRACT performance,
COMPANY is entitled to request full or partial copy of this dossier.
10 CLOSE-OUT REPORTING
The Basic Certification and the Certifying Authority shall issue to CONTRACTOR a close-out report with similar
content to that of CONTRACTOR’s close-out report but covering only the Basic Certification or the Certifying
Authority scope of WORK.
