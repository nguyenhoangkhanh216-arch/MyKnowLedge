1  BASELINE INFORMATION SECURITY CONTROLS

  General Controls

CONTRACTOR GROUP shall designate an information security point of contact within CONTRACTOR GROUP’s
organization  to  act  as  the  liaison  between  COMPANY  and  CONTRACTOR  GROUP  who  has  the  appropriate
mandate and status to represent CONTRACTOR GROUP to manage security as stated in this Information Security
Appendix.

CONTRACTOR GROUP connections to COMPANY systems shall:

•

•

use  a  secure  communication  methodology  as  agreed  with  a  qualified  technical  resource  in
COMPANY; and

use  a  secure  identification  and  authentication  mechanism  as  approved  by  a  qualified  and
authorized technical resource of COMPANY.

Incident Management and Security Reporting (IM)

CONTRACTOR GROUP shall have a documented and tested incident management plan to be enacted in the event
of an information security incident involving COMPANY data or systems. This plan shall include:

•

•

•

a process to notify COMPANY in the event of a suspected or actual compromise of COMPANY
data or systems within 48 hours of a suspected or actual breach,

analysis of the incident (including forensic investigations, analysis of logs and the audit trail,
etc.),

a process to remediate any discovered vulnerabilities in  CONTRACTOR GROUP’s systems or
processes.

  Acceptable Use Policy and Awareness

CONTRACTOR  GROUP  shall  have  a  documented  ‘Acceptable  Use  Policy’  or  ‘Code  of  Conduct’  covering
information security which is enforced against all CONTRACTOR GROUP PERSONNEL involved in the CONTRACT.
CONTRACTOR  GROUP  shall  ensure  that  all  CONTRACTOR  GROUP  PERSONNEL  are  adequately  trained  in
information security awareness prior to being engaged in the CONTRACT.

2

SPECIFIC INFORMATION SECURITY CONTROLS

  PERSONNEL Screening

CONTRACTOR  GROUP  shall  ensure  (and  shall  evidence  to  COMPANY  on  request)  that  CONTRACTOR  GROUP
PERSONNEL  who  are  responsible  for:  handling  confidential  information;  managing  highly  critical  systems
(including industrial control systems), applications and networks as part of the CONTRACT have (as a minimum)
had all relevant national police clearance prior to being engaged in the CONTRACT.

  Physical Security

CONTRACTOR GROUP shall ensure that premises that store or process COMPANY data are secured with effective
physical security controls compliant or equivalent to international standard ISO 27001: 2013 or NIST CSF.

Physical access to CONTRACTOR GROUP facilities used to store or process COMPANY data shall be provided only
to authorized CONTRACTOR GROUP PERSONNEL on a need-to-have basis. CONTRACTOR GROUP shall keep clear
records of access to its facilities and shall control the set of personnel granted physical access.

Cloud Services

If ‘Cloud Services’ is part of the CONTRACT, CONTRACTOR GROUP shall comply with NOC Cloud Baseline Security
Controls.

Software Development and Configuration

If ‘Software Development and Configuration’ is part of the CONTRACT, CONTRACTOR GROUP shall comply with
this sub-Clause 2.4.

“fixes” shall be supplied in a timeframe approved by COMPANY representative; and

have  a  change/configuration  management  system  in  place  that  governs  the  integrity  of  its
systems and software delivered to COMPANY.

Industrial Control Systems, Including SCADA

If Industrial Control Systems assets such as supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) systems, distributed
control systems (DCS), and other control system configurations such as Programmable Logic Controllers (PLC),
Human  Machine  Interfaces  (HMI),  Engineering/Operator  workstations  etc.  is  part  of  the  CONTRACT,
CONTRACTOR GROUP shall comply with this sub-Clause 2.5.

CONTRACTOR GROUP shall:

•

•

•

•

•

protect  the  confidentiality  and  integrity  of  all  the  information  related  to  COMPANY  and/or
received from COMPANY and shall ensure that CONTRACTOR GROUP PERSONNEL providing
services on Industrial Control Systems (ICS) are fully  aware of all safety regulations of both
CONTRACTOR GROUP and COMPANY;

ensure  that  the  cybersecurity  features  provided  shall  not  adversely  impact  the  required
performance (including response time), required effectiveness, required reliability or required
operation of functions important to safety of the ICS systems in COMPANY;

not  propose  nor  provide  any  control  system  or  system  component  that  the  CONTRACTOR
GROUP is not committed to supporting for a period defined by COMPANY, from the date of
commercial operation of the system. Any third-party components provided with the supplied
systems shall be covered in the CONTRACTOR GROUP’s support commitment

use an implementation that complies with the current applicable interoperability and security
standards,  as  specified  by  COMPANY  including  the  ISA/IEC  62443,  ISO/IEC  27019:2017  and
Qatar National ICS/SCADA Security Standard;

upon  COMPANY’s  request,  the  CONTRACTOR  GROUP  shall  return  or  document  the  secure
disposal of the COMPANY data and COMPANY-owned hardware that is no longer needed by
the CONTRACTOR GROUP;

room, CCTV coverage with access provided to a select and authorized group of individuals);

ensure that any systems and connection types used to connect to COMPANY ICS systems shall
be subject to a risk assessment by COMPANY, prior to the connection;

ensure that any removable media, used for the purpose of the CONTRACT in consideration, is
approved by COMPANY representative and scanned using a COMPANY approved anti-malware
solution;

and

notify COMPANY as soon as possible if any suspicious activity is discovered.
