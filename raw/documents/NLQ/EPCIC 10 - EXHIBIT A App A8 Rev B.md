1 SCOPE
This appendix details CONTRACTOR’s duties and deliverables regarding the minimum requirements for training
of COMPANY personnel in accordance with Work Unit 10.3 9.3 of EXHIBIT A.
2 CONTRACTOR DUTIES
CONTRACTOR shall prepare a Training Program for APPROVAL, and based on the APPROVED program, train
COMPANY personnel. The program should begin during the detailed ENGINEERING engineering phase and be
completed prior to Commissioning.
The Training Program should include design principles, operating modes/strategies/ maintenance. Emphasis
should be on those activities that are least understandable from manuals namely, START-Upstart-up, shutdown
and troubleshooting. All disciplines should be included, specifically instruments and controls, electrical,
tTelecom, HVAC, gGalley aAppliances and mechanical. Training for all disciplines and personnel should be
completed before Transfer of Operation and Maintenance (TOM) to COMPANY.
The Training Program shall be designed to provide the adequate level of competency for positions of
management, sections heads, supervisors, operators, and maintenance personnel.
The Training Program will be developed in accordance with the following minimum requirements:
i. Contractor CONTRACTOR must develop a detailed technical training plan for Company COMPANY
operations personnel for the contract CONTRACT equipment. A comprehensive training schedule must
be provided for Company COMPANY review and approval APPROVAL with tenderCONTRACTOR. The
schedule must be submitted for approval APPROVAL by Company COMPANY to permit issue for
implementation no later than 6 months post Contract CONTRACT award and shall include each vendor’s
VENDOR’s specific equipment and training delivery schedule and agreed participant plan 6 months post
Contract CONTRACT award.
ii. All training modules including syllabus, schedules for delivery, material to be presented, material to be
provided to participants and competency assessment modules to be undertaken shall be agreed in
advance with CompanyCOMPANY. All materials should be available for review no less than 3 months
prior to each scheduled course. Contractor CONTRACTOR and its vendors VENDORs should anticipate
Company COMPANY review and comment and must anticipate and allow for time and resources to
update prior to final approval APPROVAL to deliver training.
iii. The training schedule must be integrated fully with the Contractor's CONTRACTOR’s overall delivery
schedule. The plan must be developed for each system and equipment item/type or package as
appropriate and, appropriate to the item. The plan shall address both classroom and field components:
Classroom − Description of equipment
o Normal startup, shutdown and operation
o Routine maintenance tasks
o Specialist Maintenance and troubleshooting tasks (per discipline – Instruments and
controls, electrical, Ttelecom, HVAC, Ggalley Aappliances and mechanical )
Demonstration / Simulation :
o Subcontractor SUBCONTRACTOR / Vendor VENDOR Workshop WORKSHOP −
Familiarisation of equipment in Vendors VENDORs works (including key assembly /
disassembly opportunities, equipment operation)
o Yard / Site SITE − Familiarisation of equipment on facility in construction yards
The training plan shall include:
o Duration of each training course;
o Intended locations (and options);

iv. The Training Program will include theoretical phases, where, fundamental principles of the operation
of SYSTEMS and detailed operating procedures shall be explained, and also practical phase during which
operators shall become familiar with the FACILITIES in order to be as efficient as possible at START-
Upstart-up and during normal operations.
v. All training must be delivered by competent personnel fluent in the English language, supported by
training materials written in English prepared by competent authors. The format of training materials
must be Approved and must be suitable for editing to enable future training delivery.
vi. Contractor CONTRACTOR must appoint a training manager or lead whose role is to provide all necessary
assistance to the Specialised Training Provider. This assistance will be in the form of qualified personnel
to design and deliver training and to carry out planning logistics for training courses delivered. On
completion of each specific training session, and within one calendar week Contractor CONTRACTOR
must provide documentation to Company COMPANY which will specify, course attendees, course
objectives, content and learning outcomes. Provision must be made by Contractor CONTRACTOR for
access by Company COMPANY to ascertain information in relation to equipment design, and operating
parameters for development of training material. The Training Program will encourage the use of
Interactive training tools.
vii. CONTRACTOR shall involve qualified and experienced VENDOR Specialists where required for
completion of the training plan.
viii. The Training program will include integration of trained personnel PERSONNEL into CONTRACTOR
Commissioning team to provide FACILITIES familiarisation to such personnelPERSONNEL.
ix. The Training Program shall address issues such as duration of the Training Program, planning, location,
and the methods and techniques which shall be necessary for the program to achieve the stated
objectives.
x. The Training Program shall detail the training matrices for each category of personnel PERSONNEL
setting out the nature of the training sessions to be attended, the duration of each training session, and
the level of competency to be achieved at the end of the Training Program.
xi. CONTRACTOR shall note that the training shall be coordinated with COMPANY PLANT Operators and
not be planned by CONTRACTOR such that it conflicts with any of the WORKS. CONTRACTOR shall
submit a training plan schedule for APPROVAL thirty (30) days prior to commencement of training to
ensure COMPANY availability.
xii. Since COMPANY Offshore personnel PERSONNEL is working on a rotational basis (4 weeks on x 4 weeks
off), CONTRACTOR shall arrange enough training sessions in such a way that all COMPANY personnel
PERSONNEL can attend the training.
CONTRACTOR shall supply all training aids.
CONTRACTOR shall define a training organisation at an early stage of the WORKS upon formal request from
COMPANY, to clearly define the methods for preparation and implementation of the Training Program and
competency evaluation of personnelPERSONNEL.
Upon completion of the Training Program, CONTRACTOR shall prepare a Training Close-out Report which
outlines the training activities completed, attendance records, competency evaluation records and any other
deliverables prepared as part of the program.
