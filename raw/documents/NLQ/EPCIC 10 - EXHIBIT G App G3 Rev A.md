4.3 Activity Weight Factor
4.3.1 The weight factor is a percentage value which reflects the relative effort required to perform, or the
value assigned to, an activity within a planning level.
4.3.2 For higher level constituents (i.e. relevant Work Packages, Work Units of the WBS), the weight factors
shall be based on value and will be weighted in proportion to its cost expressed as a percentage of the
CONTRACT PRICE.
4.3.3 For lower planning levels, weight factors will be calculated by:
i. Planned man-hours for engineering activities.
ii. Value for activities such as procurement of equipment and materials.
iii. Physical quantities and man-hours for fabrication.
iv. On- and Off-shore pre-commissioning and commissioning phases weight factors shall be
in accordance with ICAPS breakdown.
4.3.4 A weight factor is to be allocated to each direct activity.
4.3.5 At all planning levels, the sum of weight factors shall equal 100%.
4.3.6 CONTRACTOR shall, as part of its planning, scheduling, and progress measurement and control
procedure, submit a table of relative weight factors for the activities. These weight factors shall be

5 PROGRESS CONTROL
5.1 Progress Monitoring System
5.1.1 A progress control system shall provide, at each cut-off date, the planned and actual progress at any
planning level.
5.1.2 It shall enable:
i. The evaluation of the quantity of WORK performed compared with the quantity of WORK
planned.
ii. The adaptation of the level of resources for modulating/accelerating the performance of
each activity, as required by the evaluation of the planning.
iii. The analysis of variations to implement corrective actions, if necessary.
iv. The follow-up of the planned man-hours and the tracking of expended man-hours to
control productivity.
v. The assessment of ongoing activities and activities remaining duration.
5.1.3 Productivity Factors and Productivity
5.1.3.1 To evaluate the remaining duration of all ‘in-progress’ activities, a productivity factor is to be
identified for each discipline.
5.1.3.2 The productivity factor for any work is obtained by dividing the planned man-hours by the man-hours
expended to achieve it.
i. A factor <1 demonstrates low productivity.
ii. A factor >1 demonstrates high productivity.
5.1.3.3 Productivity factor follow-up is illustrated by a discipline level curve, issued by CONTRACTOR to
COMPANY and updated at schedule update.
5.1.3.4 Productivity factors shall also be used to update actual manpower levels per discipline.
5.1.3.5 CONTRACTOR shall keep up-to-date records and inform COMPANY each month regarding man-hours
rates (number of man-hours expended per quantity of material installed) and productivity of the work
force. Such productivity shall be monitored at the Sub Work Unit level or as a breakdown of the Sub
Work Unit.
5.1.3.6 Actual figures shall be compared with planned figures for the month and to-date. And, if necessary,
planned staffing levels shall be adjusted to meet the scheduled COMPLETION DATES.
5.1.4 S-curves and Progress Tables
5.1.4.1 Early planned, late planned and actual progress at activity level shall be consolidated and rolled-up to
discipline level, and Sub Work Unit and Work Unit levels. Work Unit level progress figures resulting
from such consolidation shall be further rolled-up to Work Package level and ultimately to PROJECT
level.
5.1.4.2 Early and late planned, and actual progress values shall be expressed through progress S-curves and
progress tables.
5.1.4.3 CONTRACTOR shall issue progress S-curves at Work Package, Work Unit, and Sub Work Unit levels.
COMPANY may request additional S-curves to cover specific work package/WU/combination of
scopes spilt; such request may be discussed before finalization of progress curves or during the
execution of Project.
