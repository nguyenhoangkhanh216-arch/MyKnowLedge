1 SCOPE
This Appendix further details CONTRACTOR’s duties and deliverables regarding Regulatory Compliance. It shall
be read in conjunction with Article 23 of the AGREEMENT, and Clause 4.8 ‘Regulatory compliance’ of EXHIBIT A.
2 OBJECTIVES
The regulatory and environmental objectives for the WORK are that the:
i. PLANT is designed, constructed, installed, and commissioned in a manner which
incorporates regulatory and environmental requirements.
ii. PLANT complies with the SPECIFICATIONS and with all applicable government regulations,
statutes, and permit requirements.
iii. WORK is performed in a manner consistent and compatible with COMPANY's standards
(EXHIBIT E) and business objectives, including:
a. Performance of the WORK in an environmentally sound manner,
b. Definition and communication of performance requirements,
c. Personnel training, qualification, and ability to perform the WORK,
d. Performance monitoring, using a performance assessment system which
provides feedback for correction of deficiencies in performance of the WORK,
e. Performance of the WORK in a way that protects workers and the public from
injuries and controls emissions and wastes at or below environmentally
acceptable levels,
f. Demonstration of management leadership and commitment with accountability
at all organisational levels to encourage active and personal roles in complying
with regulatory requirements and environmental standards.
iv. There is full compliance with applicable Local and International laws at the WORKSITE(S)
resulting in:
a. No issuance of non-compliance notices from governing authorities.
b. No environmental incidents.

3 CONTRACTOR DUTIES
CONTRACTOR shall:
i. Be responsible for all regulatory and environmental aspects of the WORK, including WORK
performed by its SUBCONTRACTORS.
ii. Comply with all relevant government regulations and laws regarding performance of the
WORK.
iii. Develop, document, and implement regulatory and environmental policies consistent
with COMPANY's standards provided in EXHIBIT E.
iv. Consult with COMPANY on regulatory requirements, as requested or required.
v. Develop, publish, and enforce project regulatory and environmental standards for the
WORK.
vi. Identify and obtain all necessary approvals, licenses and permits required to execute the
WORK. CONTRACTOR will consult with COMPANY regarding responsibility for obtaining
required regulatory approvals if previously unanticipated requirements are identified.
vii. Meet all conditions of agreements and permits with various applicable government
authorities, including rectification of inconsistencies between conditions of different
permits prior to the start of the WORK.
viii. Supply information to COMPANY as required for timely submittal of permits for which
COMPANY is responsible.
ix. Conduct environmental analyses, studies, and assessments for all relevant phases of the
WORK, according to regulating requirements and COMPANY’s internal requirements to
implement agreed Regulatory and Environmental Program.
x. Develop and conduct regulatory and environmental training programs for supervisory
personnel and employees regarding safe and environmentally sound construction
practices, regulatory requirements, safety, health, and environmental awareness, specific
site hazards, and potential environmental impacts and mitigation measures.
xi. Develop and implement a regulatory and environmental monitoring program for the
WORK.
xii. Prepare and issue monthly regulatory and environmental reports to COMPANY as part of
the monthly progress report.
xiii. Resolve audit findings from regulatory agencies or COMPANY promptly and to
COMPANY's satisfaction.
xiv. Provide COMPANY with a detailed description of its regulatory and environmental
organisation.
xv. Provide COMPANY with documented environmental and regulatory risk assessment
procedures and results for CONTRACTOR's risk assessments.
xvi. Develop and implement environmental monitoring programs, as requested in
EXHIBIT L.
xvii. Develop, implement, and maintain Regulatory and Environmental Management Plans to
systemically manage compliance with all applicable regulatory requirements,
requirements established by COMPANY in absence of regulations, and environmental
standards. CONTRACTOR shall monitor its performance against these Plans.
xviii. Evaluate the effectiveness of its Plans and, when necessary, make the appropriate
modifications to the Plans.

4 TYPICAL CONTENT FOR REGULATORY COMPLIANCE PLAN
i. Introduction/Overview: The Regulatory Compliance Plan (RCP) shall describe the
processes that the CONTRACTOR will have in place to ensure that all applicable laws and
regulations are followed during performance of the WORK.
ii. Scope and Objectives. The purpose of the CONTRACTOR's RCP shall be to describe the
methods, processes, timing, and actions that the CONTRACTOR will use to limit incidents
of non-compliance and schedule impacts.
iii. Reference Documents and Control: The RCP will define how it is linked to other relevant
documents and define how it will be administered, distributed, and controlled.
iv. Expectations: Defines CONTRACTOR specific regulatory compliance expectations.
v. Organisation: CONTRACTOR's regulatory compliance organisation includes staffing
requirements for planning and implementing the RCP. The RCP shall identify
CONTRACTOR personnel that will be responsible for ensuring that activities are in full
compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. CONTRACTOR must demonstrate that
it has allocated sufficient resources and time to accomplish the tasks identified in this
document. Roles and responsibilities shall be clearly defined and documented for site
management, supervisory personnel, and the workforce. The RCP shall also describe how
roles and responsibilities are communicated to personnel.
vi. Procedures:
a. Process for identifying relevant laws and regulations: The RCP shall describe the
process that the CONTRACTOR will follow to ensure that all applicable laws,
regulations and treaties (including local, national and international) and
associated permits, licenses, approvals, notifications etc., are identified.
b. Listing of all permits, licenses, approvals, notifications and associated laws and
regulations that are relevant to the WORK. This includes permits/approvals such
as, but not limited to the following:
1. Permit to operate as an oil service contractor.
2. Permits for service boats.
3. Import of equipment and materials.
4. Work permits and visas.
5. Communications.
6. Notification to the Navy of arrival of installations, vessels.
7. Industrial consumer (storage) license (to store fuel).
8. Effluent discharge permits.
c. The RCP shall list all applicable government regulations, including permits
required for any aspect of the WORK. These shall include, but shall not be limited
to:
1. Labour laws.
2. Tax Laws.
3. Contracting Laws.
4. Customs Laws.
5. Laws governing offshore operations.
6. Laws governing workplace/occupational health and safety.
7. Laws governing emissions and discharges to the environment.
8. Laws governing waste disposal.
