Objective and Scope

The  objective  of  this  Exhibit  is  to  define  the  process  and  methodology  for  the  Handover  of  Interface  Items
(Material and/or equipment), long-lead item (LLI) and Goods together with the associated documentation during
Project  Execution  by  insuring  traceable  and  smooth  transfer  of  Goods  and  Interface  Items  (Material  and/or
equipment)  from  the  Supplying  CONTRACTOR  to  the  Receiving  CONTRACTOR  or  COMPANY  In-Country
Warehouse.

This standard applies to all Goods and Interface Items Handover which occur during Project Execution. Three (3)
main processes of Goods and Interface Items Handover are part of the scope of this Exhibit:

1) Handover from “Supplying CONTRACTOR” to “Receiving CONTRACTOR”,

2) Handover from “Supplying CONTRACTOR” to “COMPANY In-Country Warehouse”.

5.1.2 Definition of Item Category (Category A or Category B)

The definition of Item Category shall follow the following guidelines:



Category B Items: any untagged Item unlikely to be damaged during transportation assuming
that  necessary  preservation  measures  are  correctly  implemented.  Typically,  this  Category
includes Bolts and Nuts, Gaskets, Fittings, Flanges, Pipes, Hub Clamps, Rolled sections, Plates,
Electrical and Instrumentation cables, Electrical and Instrumentation bulk. Note that Category
B  Items  shall  have  been  inspected  first  at  CONTRACTOR  WORKSITE  and  any  Item  directly
shipped from VENDOR to Qatar cannot be considered as Category B Item.



Category A Items: any other item not identified as a Category B Item.

5.1.3 Unique Identification Number

The Supplying CONTRACTOR shall attribute to each Interface Item or group of identical Interface Item a Unique
Identification Number. Such numbering shall be submitted to COMPANY for APPROVAL.

5.1.4 Dispatch Dossier Preparation

For each Interface Item or group of identical Interface Items, a Dispatch Dossier will have to be created by the
Supplying CONTRACTOR. The identification of the Dispatch Dossiers will be done through the aforementioned
Unique Identification Number, and Dispatch Dossiers will be the compilation of all the documentation required
by  COMPANY  Warehouse  SAP  system  called  “QC  Codes”.  COMPANY  shall  give  the  Dispatch  Dossier  Indexes
(listing  QC  Codes  required  to  be  part  of  the  Dispatch  Dossier)  to  the  Supplying  CONTRACTOR  based  on  the
Material Take off provided by the Supplying CONTRACTOR. After compilation and record in Sharefile (or in the
Interface Management Software if requested by COMPANY or formally accepted by COMPANY) of such Dispatch
Dossiers, the Supplying CONTRACTOR shall notify COMPANY for review. Only after APPROVAL of corresponding
Dispatch Dossiers will the Supplying CONTRACTOR be entitled to notify COMPANY for Interface Item Release
Inspection.

both  Dispatch  Dossiers  review  and  Release  Inspection,  an  IRN  will  be  issued  by  COMPANY,  this  IRN  being  a
prerequisite prior transfer to COMPANY In-Country Warehouse.
