1. SCOPE
Project Risk Management (PRM) is the process to manage and report the Threats and/or Opportunities that
could affect the successful outcome of the PROJECT in terms of delivery, schedule, cost, HSE/security, quality,
performance, and other objectives.
CONTRACTOR shall monitor Risks in its own scope of WORK, including that of its SUBCONTRACTORS and within
COMPANY’s activities which could affect CONTRACTOR.
The PRM methodology should consider both the positive (opportunities) and negative (threat) Risk events and
uncertainties.
2. RISK MANAGEMENT PLAN
CONTRACTOR shall establish and maintain a Risk-averse approach and formalize it in a Risk Management Plan
which shall be submitted to COMPANY for APPROVAL. It shall be in accordance with the minimum principles and
requirements set forth in the CONTRACT DOCUMENTS, and in particular, this EXHIBIT G, Appendix G5.
i. The Risk Management Plan shall, as a minimum, address/contain the following elements:
ii. Detailed Risk management activities to be carried out;
iii. Methodology, ensuring consistent application throughout the process;
iv. CONTRACTOR and Stakeholder roles and responsibilities;
v. Methods or plan to ensure participation and compliance with the process;
vi. Demonstrate how the CONTRACTOR intends to eliminate Risk and optimizing opportunities through
means of a common understanding of objectives;
vii. Comply to the required periodic review frequency and reporting formats.

3. RESPONSIBILITY
CONTRACTOR is responsible for its Risks and reporting of said Risks under the Risk management process and
does not imply a transfer of liability to the COMPANY.
CONTRACTOR shall:
i. Implement an APPROVED Risk Management Plan;
ii. Ensure to identify, evaluate & report all Risks (both threats and opportunities), including those from
SUBCONTRACTORs/VENDORs;
iii. Develop an effective and comprehensive Mitigation plan, in consultation of CONTRACTOR’s internal
subject matter experts / specialist and all the relevant stakeholders, including the respective Risk Owners
& Action Owners;
iv. CONTRACTOR shall provide a Risk Report, including the Risk Register, which contains the identified Risk
and its mitigation status on a monthly basis;
v. Upon COMPANY APPROVAL of the Detailed Work Time Schedule (DWTS), the CONTRACTOR shall
organize and perform a Planning and Schedule Risk Analysis (PSRA) as set out in Section 10 below.
CONTRACTOR shall engage a qualified third-party or utilize a competent independent CONTRACTOR
resource, both which shall be APPROVED by COMPANY. The CONTRACTOR shall organize a dedicated
debrief following the PSRA, and formally present and submit for COMPANY comment, the findings in the
form of a PSRA Report.
4. ORGANIZATION
CONTRACTOR shall nominate a person, identified as the ‘Risk Manager’, responsible for the Risk management
process and activity. This person shall be the primary point of contact for COMPANY regarding Risk procedures,
Risk information, and reporting. CONTRACTOR shall propose for COMPANY APPROVAL CVs or prospective
personnel to fulfil the role. The Risk Manager shall be proficient with industry-recognised Risk management
principles and methodologies.

6. IDENTIFICATION
Risks shall be identified according to PROJECT phase, Work Package, Work Unit, and Sub Work Unit with a
description of Risk Event, causes and consequences. It is CONTRACTOR’s responsibility to ensure that all Risks,
both opportunities and threats as per the CONTRACT Scope of Work, including SUBCONTRACTOR/VENDOR, are
identified and mitigated in accordance with CONTRACT EXHIBIT G, Appendix G5.
CONTRACTOR should have a clear separation between “Risk” and “Issues”. The key difference for an “Issue” is
that it is an event that have already has occurred, while a “Risk” is an uncertain or potential event that may or
may not happen. Issues should be maintained separately in an Issue Log.
Members of the PROJECT team, along with other stakeholders, shall be consulted for Initial threats and
opportunity identification. Two (2) types of risk should be identified: Project Risk Level and Unit Risk Level.
To systematically identify PROJECT risks, the follow activities shall be undertaken by the CONTRCATOR:
i. Review PROJECT documents, plans and budgets.
ii. Include and review CONTRACTOR’s previous lesson learnt and previous Risk assessments, whichever
relevant;
iii. Conduct brainstorming sessions.
iv. Interview members and stakeholders of the PROJECT.
v. Carry-out workshop with SUBCONTRACTORS and/or COMPANY’s interface Entities/Other parties and
stakeholders.
vi. Identify key events and decisions where risk and/or opportunity may be introduced or eliminated.
vii. Identify, consolidate & monitor critical threats and/or opportunity events from other CONTRACTOR
workshops including, but not limited to the following: 90% Model Review, HAZID, HAZOP, SIL,
Constructability, SIMOPS and T&I workshops.
All CONTRACTOR project members are encouraged to identify and communicate potential to line management
and/or Risk Manager throughout the duration of the PROJECT.

7. EVALUATION
CONTRACTOR shall establish an evaluation system to score the probability and impact of each Risk item based
on this Section 7 and the Risk matrices included herein.
The evaluation of an individual Risk is by:
i. Detailing the Risk event, cause and consequences;
ii. Assessments referring to the typical Risk Breakdown Structure (RBS) aspects, such as:
a. Social Political Risk: Risks related to THIRD PARTIES (local communities, local government, and
state government). It covers the social/labor/union or workers protest movements, the security
for personnel aspects as the changes in laws/regulations regarding the personnel/workforce
employment;
b. Technologic Risk: Risks related to the performances of the WORK (with reference to the
contractual scope of WORK), such as DESIGN DOSSIER compliance, technological uncertainties
and evolutions (e.g., non-proven technology, mandatory upgrades due to external inputs) or
external to PROJECT resources availability;
c. Organizational Risk: Risks related to PROJECT activities coordination, communication
dysfunctions, and interfaces management (between PROJECT teams within CONTRACTOR’s
organization and with COMPANY and COMPANY’s other contractors, with THIRD PARTIES);
d. Suppliers: Risks directly attributable to SUBCONTRACTORS in the performance of their activities
(such as resources mobilization, fulfillment of contractual requirements, subcontract
management, supplier bankruptcy);
e. HSE/Security: Risks linked to one or more of the HSE aspects, including security and related
commitments fulfillment.
iii. Assessing based on the various Project phases (Engineering, Procurement, Construction, Installation
& Commissioning) impacted;
iv. Probability of occurrence;
v. Impacts in terms of Severity: Human, Production, Financial, Environmental, Reputation/
Compliance & Schedule delay;
vi. Manageability based on mitigation strategy and plan;
vii. Scoring and ranking based on Overall Impact Value [Probability Score * Severity Score].
viii. Maintain a clear segregation between Risks and Issues.
The scoring and ranking of Risk will be done in the following two (2) steps based on the 6 x 6 risk matrix presented
in Table 7-1:
1. Overall Impact: In order to refine the ranking of each Risks, the formula here after will be used:
Overall Impact = Probability Score * Severity Score
2. Criticality Ranking: The ranking assigned to the Risk Value based on the following ranges:
a. 1 –6 Minor
b. 8-18 Significant
c. 24-64 Unacceptable
3. Risk Value: In the event where there are an actual representation or quantified value of the
Criticality (mainly cost and/or schedule), that value should be described.

8. MITIGATION
The Project Manager and the Risk Manager identify which strategy is best for each Risk, and then design specific
actions to implement that strategy, with the consensus of the relevant stakeholders.
CONTRACTOR shall provide a statement for each identified Risk item describing the way Risk will be mitigated
and managed, with the planned Start and Completion dates for each Risk. It identifies and assigns Action Owner
to take responsibility for each Risk response, this way it instills ownership and ensures the Risk is reduced to an
acceptable level. The Risk Manager and the Action Owner should provide a detailed status against each
mitigation based on the actions that was implemented, during the dedicated Risk Review sessions.
These strategies and actions shall include:
i. Avoidance: Eliminate the risk by changing scope, adding time or adding resources.
ii. Transfer: Transfer the impact of risk.
iii. Mitigation: Reduce the probability or consequences of a Risk event to an acceptable
threshold.
iv. Acceptance: Accept the risk.
Mitigations should be specific actions that are to be implemented in mitigating any Risks, which should be clearly
separated from existing safeguards or standard project execution / tasks / activities.

CONTRACTOR shall document and establish its procedures/methods for follow up and close-out of each Risk
