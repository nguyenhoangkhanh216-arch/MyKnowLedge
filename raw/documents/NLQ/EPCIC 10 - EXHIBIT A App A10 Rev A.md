1

General

Interface  organisation  and  identification  are  within  CONTRACTOR’s  scope  as  detailed  below  in  Clause  4
(Interfaces  Definition,  Matrix  and  Procedure).  Follow-up  and  performance  of  identified  tasks  related  to
Interfaces are an integral part of CONTRACTOR scope of WORK.

Interface management is within CONTRACTOR’s scope of WORK shall be executed from EFFECTIVE DATE until
issuance of the COMPLETION CERTIFICATE. Interface Management covers all CONTRACTOR activities including
engineering,  procurement,
installation,  hook-up  and
fabrication,  pre-commissioning,  transportation,
commissioning.

2

Interface Management Principles

CONTRACTOR shall ensure early identification of interfaces in accordance with the scope of WORK and battery
limits.

CONTRACTOR  shall  collaborate  with  all  Parties  involved  in  interface  activities  associated  with  the  WORK,
through COMPANY, and ensure that technical assistance to interfaces is dealt with in a prompt and efficient
manner. CONTRACTOR shall gather and coordinate the required information to allow its prompt, reliable and
traceable exchange for the benefit of all interfacing parties.

CONTRACTOR  shall  issue  deliverables  (as  specified  in  EXHIBIT  C)  as  requested  by  the  other  Parties  and  be
responsible for the review of all deliverables associated with the interface to assist COMPANY and ensure that
the final design is in accordance with his own engineering WORK, and that all aspects of the WORK are coherent.

COMPANY shall always remain responsible of overall Management of Interfaces.

3

Interface Management Process

CONTRACTOR shall provide a follow-up and reporting tool on all interfaces-related matters, and critical items
shall be reported in the project monthly report. In addition, CONTRACTOR shall formally advise COMPANY of
any interface problems which may arise.

CONTRACTOR  shall  channel  all  communications  with  COMPANY's  other  contractors  through  COMPANY.
Accordingly,  CONTRACTOR  shall  implement  appropriate  related  procedures  and  submit  to  COMPANY  for
APPROVAL an Interface Plan, schedule, Interface Coordination Procedures and the overall and detailed drawings
and/or tables as appropriate showing the physical interfaces. This includes preparation of the list of information
needed by CONTRACTOR and the dates at which this information is requested from other parties.

The requirements in respect of interfaces including engineering are also applicable in relation to CONTRACTOR
ITEMS to be supplied by CONTRACTOR for COMPANY’s OTHER CONTRACTORS.

CONTRACTOR  shall  organise  Interface  kick-off  meeting  within  the  first  month  after  award  and  subsequent
weekly Technical interface co-ordination meetings.

CONTRACTOR  shall  clearly  define  and  allocate  dedicated  resources  for  interface  management  within  the
CONTRACTOR’s  scope.  CONTRACTOR  shall  designate  a  focal  point  for  all  interface  matters  and  identify  an
Interface Leader, together with roles and responsibilities.

COMPANY  will  provide  an  interface  management  IT  tool  which  CONTRACTOR  must  use  for  interface
management.
