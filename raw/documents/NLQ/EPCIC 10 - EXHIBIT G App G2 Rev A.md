4

EVALUATION OF A CHANGE

4.1

Price Adjustments to CHANGE ORDERS

4.1.1

Effects  of  CHANGE  ORDERS  on  the  CONTRACT  PRICE  shall  be  evaluated  by  CONTRACTOR  using  the
following methods, in order of priority:

i.

ii.

iii.

iv.

Comparison with, or analogy/interpolation to, lump sum(s) in EXHIBIT B.

Use of unit rates in EXHIBIT B.

Use of time rates in EXHIBIT B.

If none of the above methods are applicable/feasible, then the value shall be established
by reference to the nearest of the prices or rates set-out in EXHIBIT B.

v.

For material purchase, the cost will be calculated on the basis of actual Purchase Order.

4.1.2

Refer to the corresponding articles in EXHIBIT B for further definition.

4.2

Schedule Adjustment

4.2.1

COMPANY  requires  CONTRACTOR  to adhere to  the due dates  in EXHIBIT  C, WORK  TIME SCHEDULE.
Consequently, any schedule extension should be avoided to the maximum possible extent by increases
in  workforce,  overtime  or  any  other  means  necessary  to  maintain  the  WORK  TIME  SCHEDULE.  A
detailed schedule adjustment shall be made on the basis of existing Project schedules in order to show
changes in tasks performance, duration, timing, sequence and resources, and assess any consequence
to the PROJECT Schedule.

4.2.2

COMPLETION DATES shall not be adjusted unless the affected WORK is on the critical path and no float
exists in the WORK TIME SCHEDULE.
