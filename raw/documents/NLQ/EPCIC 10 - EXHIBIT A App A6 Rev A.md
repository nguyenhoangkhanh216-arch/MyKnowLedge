1 GENERAL ORGANISATION
CONTRACTOR shall conduct at early stage of the preparation phase a comprehensive gap analysis with
COMPANY procedures relevant to lifting activities. CONTRACTOR shall submit this gap analysis to COMPANY and
demonstrate that its lifting Management System is compliant with COMPANY standards for the execution of the
WORK.
CONTRACTOR lifting policy, procedures and lifting Risk Assessments shall be APPROVED by COMPANY.
Lifting equipment to be used at WORKSITES shall be duly certified by a recognised authority (refer to Exhibit J).
The certificates shall be made available to COMPANY, Marine Warranty Surveyor and Third Party Authority
(TPA), as required. CONTRACTOR shall appoint a dedicated lifting superintendent with the required certification,
competence and authority, as specified in SD-NOC-LH-001.
Lifting superintendent, equipment operators, banksman, riggers and rigging loft management responsible CVs
and certificates will be evaluated by COMPANY lifting specialist and approved by CPY Lifting Technical Authority.
COMPANY shall have the right to request CONTRACTOR to change its or its SUBCONTRACTORS’ personnel having
insufficient competency for the position.
At COMPANY’s SITE, CONTRACTOR shall follow the lifting policy of COMPANY and COMPANY’s applicable
procedures and practices (refer to Exhibit E App. E3).
Lifting operations shall, as a minimum, be compliant with NSL’s International Lifting and Rigging Handbook, SD-
NOC-LH-001 Rigging and Lifting Operations, CP-NOC-057 Management of Lifting and Handling Operations.
For any lifting work from permanent cranes or offshore cranes, CONTRACTOR shall ensure all crane operators
are certified by a recognised third party to an agreed high competence standard, as specified in SD-NOC-LH-001.
2 LIFTING OPERATION ON CONTRACTOR WORKSITE
The requirements of COMPANY SD-NOC-STR-411 shall apply.
Criticality of all lifting operations shall be assessed by CONTRACTOR using Lifting Operation Criticality
Assessment table in Attachment 1 to this Appendix.
CONTRACTOR shall be able to demonstrate that generic lifting and handling procedures are in place to cover
non-critical operations.
CONTRACTOR shall issue within sixty (60) days after the EFFECTIVE DATE (ED) its list of critical lifting operations
(with regards to the construction sequences) together with the required documentation level and the
approximate date of commencement for COMPANY review, comment, and APPROVAL.
CONTRACTOR shall issue a Lifting Matrix addressing the different types of lifting (Non critical and critical
operations) that will be run at CONTRACTOR WORKSITE, with the list of required documents, timing for
COMPANY notification and the control measures to be applied. This lifting matrix will be reviewed by COMPANY
and will be included within the CONTRACTOR Lifting Plan. Example of Training Matrix is provided in Attachment
4.
3 LIFTING OPERATION ON COMPANY SITE
The requirements of COMPANY SD-NOC-STR-401 (load-out, sea-fastening, transportation and installation of
offshore structures) and COMPANY SD-NOC-STR-404 (marine aspects for load-out, transportation and
installation of offshore and onshore structures), SD-NOC-LH-001 and SD-NOC-LH-003 shall apply.
For all SITE lifting activities, lifting equipment and lifting gears shall be certified by an external recognised
accredited third party.
In addition, the criticality assessment methodology as defined in Attachment 1 of this Appendix A6 shall apply.
All non-critical lifting operation shall have:
i. Non critical lifting plan (refer to Attachment 2 for typical non critical lifting plan).
ii. Generic risk assessment.

4 LIFTING COMPETENCIES AND SUPERVISION
CONTRACTOR shall identify within its organisation, a “Competent Person in Lifting Operation” (CPLO) who shall
have sufficient knowledge and experience of lifting operations and who shall act as a reference for all topics
related to lifting, including review and validation by CONTRACTOR of its lifting documentation before submission
to COMPANY for APPROVAL. He/she shall also perform periodic reviews and inspections of lifting activities at
LOCATIONS.
During lifting/movement operation, CONTRACTOR CPLO or approved competent person will be present to make
sure that lifting/movement is performed as per approved studies.
CONTRACTOR shall develop a process to ensure efficient risk control prior commencement of any non-routine
or critical lift to verify and record that all applicable requirements have been documented, reviewed and
approved (dimensional control report, structural integrity, rigging, etc.).
Working in the danger zone will be prohibited during all the lifting activities.
5 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS
This section defines general requirements for lifting, handling, storage and transportation of systems and units.
The following general requirements apply:
All units shall be provided with the necessary installation aids, bumper bars and/or transportation
skids/boxes/frames and protective covers required for safe handling and transportation onshore and offshore:
i. All units shall be provided with Inspected, certified, and color-coded lifting points and
attachment points with the necessary equipment to lift and secure units during land and
sea transportation, transfer between offshore vessels and on-board handling and storage.
ii. Lifting devices for all units shall be provided. This shall include but not be limited to lifting
subs, spreader bar arrangement, etc., all suitable for lifting in onshore and offshore
conditions, both in air and submerged, as required.
iii. Lifting points, load bearing structures and associated lifting devices shall be designed and
certified in accordance with the appropriate industry standards.
iv. All units subjected to sea transportation shall have designated facilities (pad eyes or
similar) for sea fastening, clearly marked with "Sea Fastening Only". All other equipment
or modules which are not suitable for transportation in baskets or containers, shall be
furnished with facilities for sea fastening as appropriate.
v. All components which are handled and/or deployed as individual units shall be balanced,
both in air and submerged to provide safe lifting and handling conditions.
vi. Skids shall be fitted with forklift pockets in accordance with lifting rules.
vii. Minor equipment (>20 kg) shall be provided with dedicated lift points for maintenance
purposes, such as threaded eye bolts or safe sling attachment points as appropriate. .

6 OPERATORS
CONTRACTOR shall ensure that all personnel involved in rigging, slinging, and lifting operations are properly
trained and qualified for the activities to be performed. CONTRACTOR shall identify such personnel and
demonstrate the adequacy of the training plan versus WORK.
All operators shall receive an adequate training specific to the appliance they use with yearly refresher. It shall
be the same in case:
i. They change for another appliance.
ii. The appliance which they are dedicated to is modified.
iii. The working conditions have been significantly modified (i.e. from WORKSITE to SITE but
with the same appliance).
In any case an authorisation to work shall be delivered by the CONTRACTOR site manager to each operator
working on site, even if the lifting operations are subcontracted.
All operators shall be certified as per local regulations.

7 LIFTING OPERATIONS
Suitable tuggers lines or tag lines operated by air tugged winches shall always be used to stabilise loads.
Outriggers shall always be extended on stationary lifts when cranes are fitted with such equipment.
The yard and environment condition must be taken into account (soil bearing, yard layout, utilities...)
Communication between signalman/lift coordinator and crane operator shall by radio or appropriate means of
communication. Hand signals can be used only in case of emergency when radio signal is lost.
The crane operator shall always be at the controls while men are suspended in a personnel basket. A safety sling
shall be secured between the basket and the crane hook in addition to the main lifting sling.
Lifting using only chain hoists/tirfors as the main lifting gear is not acceptable. The main lifting device shall be a
lifting sling/wire with the chain hoists/tirfors installed in addition for operational reasons. If using a chain block,
lever hoist etc. as part of crane lift then back up sling must be used also.
Use of equipment other that certified lifting gear to maintain equipment under suspension (spools, machinery,
etc.) is mandatory. Use of industrial wire or ropes for this purpose is strictly prohibited.
Any pad eyes used for lifting operations shall be fit-for-purpose (design, strength, etc.) shall be as per SD-NOC-
STR-401, Design review and strength calculations shall be reviewed by CPY structural engineer, fully welded and
validated by non-destructive test.
8 INSPECTION
All lifting equipment shall be provided with VENDOR’s certificate and subject to periodic thorough examination
and testing carried out by competent persons. Competent persons shall be from an independent inspection
organisation.
Specified intervals for thorough examination shall be:
i. Maximum six (6) months for lifting equipment used to lift personnel and all lifting gear.
ii. Maximum twelve (12) months for lifting appliances and other lifting equipment.
Lifting gear shall be colour coded. A new colour shall be used after every thorough examination.
Lifting equipment shall also be inspected by:
i. The relevant Department at suitable intervals between thorough examinations.
ii. The user before any use.
All lifting equipment shall receive an identification tag number for follow-up.
Dates and results of examinations and inspections shall be recorded in a logbook available on the working site.
CONTRACTOR and SUB-CONTRACTOR Soft and hard copies shall be made available at CONTRACTOR site and
VENDOR certificate shall be also available on the working site.
When a lifting appliance needs maintenance or out of inspection validity period, it shall be put in quarantine and
locked with clear written instructions.
When any lifting gear is declared unsafe after an inspection, it shall be destroyed and thrown away.
9 MISCELLANEOUS
Safe Working Load (SWL) or Working load limit (WLL) shall be indicated on lifting equipment and lifting gear.
A flashing light is required on cranes and forklift trucks when moving. All vehicles (excluding cars) shall have an
automatic reversing alarm.
Overhead cranes shall be equipped with an alarm which must be activated . when moving.
COMPANY recommends to install on mobile lifting appliance (e.g. forklift, cherry picker…) a system to prevent
collision with personnel on WORKSITE (e.g. Artificial Intelligent Detection System).

10 PERSONNEL BASKETS
Lifting personnel with Personnel Basket or cantilever basket shall be avoided and considered as the last option
when all other methods have been properly considered and evaluated.
Design of personnel baskets or cantilever basket shall be supported by a comprehensive calculation note. Their
fabrication shall be only undertaken by qualified welders. Personnel baskets shall be certified by an independent
certification Company. They shall be checked and inspected by a competent person prior to any use.
Design shall comply with BS 14502-1, ASME B 30.23 standard or other standard deemed equivalent by
COMPANY. Inspection, maintenance and thorough examination shall comply with BS 7121-2 (20212)
Crane considered for man basket lifting must be marked or equiped suitable for man riding by manufacturer as
per recognised code (API 2C, EN 13852).
11 SUBCONTRACTING
In case of subcontracting of lifting work, the same requirements as defined above are applicable and provided
to the SUBCONTRACTOR in charge of the lift prior to contract award. CONTRACTOR shall verify adequacy of
SUBCONTRACTOR lifting qualification, organisation, and equipment for compliance with the COMPANY’s
requirements and submitted for APPROVAL.
