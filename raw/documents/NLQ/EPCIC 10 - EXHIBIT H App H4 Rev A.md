1

SCOPE

Appendix  H4  defines  the  requirements  to  evaluate  and  rate  the  criticality  of  the  Units  delivered  under  the
CONTRACT. These requirements apply during the all phases of the WORK (e.g. design, engineering, procurement
and supply, construction, installation, hook-up, and commissioning).

The objective of criticality rating is to define the level, or amount of:




CONTRACTOR Quality Control (QC) and Inspection of its own activities.
Quality  Surveillance  to  be  carried-out  by  CONTRACTOR  upon  their  SUBCONTRACTORS  and
VENDORS.

The criticality rating shall be based on considering the risks represented by each Unit. These requirements are
also applicable to the SUBCONTRACTOR and VENDOR contracted by CONTRACTOR through the complete supply
chain. CONTRACTOR shall prepare and implement a criticality assessment plan, including level of Quality Control,
Inspection and Surveillance activities to be carried-out by CONTRACTOR.

2

DEFINITIONS

The Project risk level is a comparative indication of the importance of a deliverable with respect to the likelihood
and the consequence of its failure on Project objectives. The Unit risk level is a comparative indication of the
probability  and  consequences  of  non-delivery  due  to  the  relative  ‘newness’  of  the  Unit  and/or  selected
contractor.

3

CRITICALITY RATING THROUGH RISK ASSESSMENT

CONTRACTOR shall structure the deliverables in module, package, equipment, material (or services) and must
conduct risk assessment at the appropriate level, taking into account its own purchasing strategy. Naming ‘Unit’
the appropriate analysis level, the output of this assessment process shall be the criticality rate of the Unit.

3.1

Risk Assessment

Two (2) categories of risk shall be considered for assessing the overall criticality of a Unit:




Those arising during the Project phases (“Project risk level”).
Those related to the VENDOR and nature of the Unit (“Unit risk level”).

In each category, identified risks result from the combinations of the consequence (gravity) of individual possible
failures with their probability of occurrence (probability of appearance and/or of detection). The consequences
are considered versus various criteria detailed for each category; the risk level finally assigned being the worst
case assessed for each category.

3.2

Project Risk Level Assessment

CONTRACTOR shall assess the Project risk level by considering:








The position of the Unit in the Project overall process and, in particular, its progress/impact on
the schedule.
The different ‘delivery failure’ modes (e.g. late delivery, ‘unfitness for purpose’, cost over-run)
and the estimated associated probability.
The consequences of each ‘failure’ mode on the Project objectives.
The  operational  risk  factors  (HSE/Safety,  impact  on  environment,  fluid  characteristics,
availability and accessibility for maintenance).

3.3

Unit Risk Level Assessment

In order to assess the unit risk level, the CONTRACTOR shall factor in:










The complexity of the Unit.
The maturity of the design, engineering, manufacturing and installation processes, especially
for innovative products (even if limited to a minor component of the Unit).
The complexity of the realisation processes.
The workload and potential overload of the VENDOR or SUBCONTRACTOR.
The confidence level into SUBCONTRACTOR or VENDOR concerned with the Unit (HSE during
all Project phases and regular satisfactory delivery track record).
The recent industry feedback.

3.4

Methodology for Risks Levels Assessment

For  the two (2) risk  levels  defined above, CONTRACTOR shall define specific criteria based on questions and
answers  to  define  the  level  of  risks  for  each  topic  of  the  risk  assessments.  Those  typical  questionnaires  and
associated criticality rating procedure shall be communicated to COMPANY for APPROVAL. The combination of
failure  probability  and  failure  consequences  provide  a  risk  level  to  be  selected  between  ‘Very  High’,  ‘High’,
‘Moderate’ and ‘Acceptable’ for each risk level (Unit or Project); the worst failure risk level is the one assigned
to the Unit.

3.5

Criticality Rating

The  criticality  rating  assessment  is  performed  by  combining  the  Unit  risk  level  with  the  Project  risk  level
according to the following table, Table 3-1:

Table 3-1  Criticality Rating Matrix






Rating 1: Highly Critical.
Rating 2: Critical.
Rating 3: Moderately Critical.
Rating 4: Not Critical.

3.6

Other Rating Methods

If CONTRACTOR uses a different method to rate criticality, it shall be submitted to COMPANY for APPROVAL. If
the method is not approved, the method described above must be applied.

3.7

Using Criticality Ratings for CONTRACTOR Quality Surveillance Activities

The  CONTRACTOR’s  QC  Plan  for  the  Unit  and  the  CONTRACTOR  Quality  Surveillance  program  for
SUBCONTRACTORS or VENDORS concerned shall be based on the Unit criticality rating. The criticality rating shall
guide  the  level  of  the  Quality  Surveillance  pertaining  to  the  Unit;  the  higher  the  criticality  rating,  the  more
detailed and extensive the verifications shall be.

The scheduling, execution and recording of surveillance activities performed by CONTRACTOR within its own
processes is defined in EXHIBIT H, Appendix H3. The implementation of the retained criticality rating method
must be described in the overall Quality Management System (QMS) by CONTRACTOR. Based on the defined
criticality ratings, CONTRACTOR shall prepare and implement Surveillance/Inspection and Test Plan(s).
