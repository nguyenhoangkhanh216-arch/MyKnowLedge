INTRODUCTION

This  document  defines  the  requirements  for  certification  documents  for  purchased  components,  items,  and
materials for the PROJECT. The scope of this document covers the components, items and materials procured
for the PROJECT.

This Appendix applies to the documentary certification requirements for components, items and materials for
the  PROJECT  and  sets  forth  the  minimum  acceptable  standards  for  the  format,  nature  and  content  of  the
certification  supplied.  In  case  of  conflict  between  present  EXHIBIT  H,  Appendix  H2,  the  Project  Particular
Specifications (PPSs), and COMPANY SPECIFICATIONS, the most stringent requirements apply.

2

DEFINITIONS

Certifying  Authority  (CA): The  accredited  Certifying  Authority  shall  be  contracted  by  CONTRACTOR  as  an
independent Third Party and be part of the scope of WORK as an ancillary service through the CONTRACT, as
described  in  EXHIBIT  A and EXHIBIT H.  Third  Party  CA  certification  is given by an Accredited Third Party. The
Certifying Authority company shall be selected from the list of APPROVED SUBCONTRACTORS/VENDORS LIST
(Refer to EXHIBIT J, when applicable).

Certified  Copy:  A  copy  of  the  original  certificate  which  has  been  verified  against  the  original  by  either  the
Originator,  the  Certifying  Authority,  an  inspector  appointed  by  CONTRACTOR,  or  an  Independent  Inspector
acceptable to COMPANY acting on behalf of the VENDOR, and which bears their original red stamp and signature.

Independent Lifting Appliance Examiner: A competent person authorised to conduct examination and testing
of lifting appliances and gear acceptable to the Certifying Authority.

Material: Raw, manufactured or processed material.

Material Test Certificate: A document which provides and attests the results of tests on the material actually
supplied, readily identifiable to, and recording the conformity of the material with the requirements of the code,
standard or specifications given in the Purchase Order.

Original Certificate: The actual certificate, which was issued by the source manufacturer of the material and
which bears the original stamps, signatures or endorsements of all relevant parties.

Purchase Order: An order executed between CONTRACTOR, and a VENDOR or SUBCONTRACTOR to manufacture
materials or equipment in accordance with applicable statutory regulations, codes, standards, CONTRACT, and
PROJECT SPECIFICATIONS.

Source: The original place of manufacture of the material.

SUBCONTRACTOR/VENDOR:  A company-appointed to supply materials or equipment in accordance with the
terms and conditions of a Purchase Order or subcontract.

STEEL  CATEGORIES:  The  structural  members  of  the  resisting  structures  shall  be  distributed  into  four  (4)
categories of importance, mainly in regard of the consequences of their possible failure during production or
during installation of constituting parts of the facilities (lifting of packages, etc.):

Special Category: Failure of any of these members would cause total or partial destruction of the facility during
its production or destruction of parts of the facility during their installation, or they are members that cannot be
repaired.

First Category: Failure of any of these members would cause total or partial facility shutdown, but no total or
partial destruction of the facility. Repair of these members would only be made if production or installation of
the facility is stopped.

Second Category: They are members other than those classified in Special Category or First Category.

Non-Structural Members: Accessory and finishing elements, i.e. not participating in the overall strength of the
platform structures (e.g. hand-rails, stairs, ladders, grating, false flooring, etc.).

directly to the equipment manufacturer by an accredited authority acceptable to COMPANY and to the Certifying
Authority. Type test and approval certificates, together with any associated manufacturing licenses, shall be in
date at the time of dispatch of the material or equipment to CONTRACTOR.

The list of material test, type test, type approval and proof test requirements which follows is not exhaustive. It

5.2.13

Fire Safe Valves

All  valves  which  have  parts  which  can  degrade  or  deform  in  the  event  of  fire  (and  especially  those  having
elastomeric seat or seal materials), and which are to be installed in hydrocarbon or fire water service (including
hydrant valves), shall have relevant fire type test certification acceptable to COMPANY. Fire test certificates will
be supplied by VENDOR from reputable independent authority.

Fire test certification for valves shall be in accordance with the requirements of API 6FA, API 607, BS:6755 Part
II, or other relevant codes as agreed with CONTRACTOR and COMPANY. The fire test certification presented shall
be applicable to the bore, pressure rating and materials of construction of the actual valve(s) supplied. Where
such certification covers a range of valve sizes, pressure classes and body materials, the limits of this range shall
be in accordance with the applicable code(s) specified.

Hydrants in fire water service shall be tested in accordance with Appendix D of the United Kingdom Department
of Transport document, 'Survey of  Fire  Appliances, Instructions for the Guidance  of Surveyors'.  All individual
valves shall be provided with hydrostatic pressure test certificates for the body and seat.

5.2.14 Pressure Relief Devices

All pressure relief devices, including safety valves (PSVs), bursting discs and relief valves shall be of a type which
has  been  satisfactorily  type  tested  to  establish  their  discharge  capabilities  at  their  maximum  rated  design
pressures  and  temperatures.  Such  type  test  certification  shall  be  in  accordance  with  an  accepted  code  or
standard, such as API 520, and performed either in the presence of the Certifying Authority themselves, or by
an independent, accredited test laboratory whose certification is acceptable to the Certifying Authority.

5.2.15 Pressure Vessels

Pressure vessels shall be provided with a certificate of code compliance in respect of design and construction,
such as Form X or Form U-1A/A-1 Certificate of Conformance, (e.g. ASME VIII-Div. 1 or ASME VIII-Div. 2 as per
SD-NOC-PVV-211)  and  CE  Marked  when  required  by  COMPANY  Standard  (e.g.  when  part  of  a  package).  In
addition, a certificate which certifies that the vessel is suitable for use on an offshore installation shall be issued
by the Certifying Authority.

5.2.16

Lifting Appliances

All lifting gear and appliances, such as monorail, beam clamp structure, pulling post, crane, davit padeyes, lifting
lugs,  etc.,  shall  be  manufactured,  tested  by  non-destructive  testing  and  proof  load  tested,  and  certified  in
accordance with the requirements of the Certifying Authority's code for lifting appliances, or an alternative code
or codes acceptable to COMPANY and the Certifying Authority.

All  lifting  appliances  shall  be  provided  with  individual  proof  load  test  certificates  and  appropriate  deflection
reports. In addition, all lifting appliances shall be physically identified by a tag with associated SWL.



Loose Gear: All loose lifting gear, including hooks, blocks, shackles, slings, swivels, rings, chains,
etc.,  shall  have  individual  proof  load  test  certificates.  Wire  ropes  and  chains  shall  have
individual proof test and material certificates in accordance with the relevant code(s).

 Winches and Tuggers: Winches and tuggers shall be supplied with a type approval certificate

for each discrete type or arrangement.

 Man-riding Winches and Tuggers: Man-riding  winches and tuggers shall be supplied with a
type approval certificate confirming that the arrangement is of a type approved for man-riding.
